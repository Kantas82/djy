<p>【单字/词解析】<br />
● spread，动词/spred/扩散<br />
自然发音：SPREAD<br />
英解：to (cause to) cover, reach, or have an effect on a wider or increasing area<br />
例句：Coronavirus may be spread through droplets transmission. （中共病毒（俗称新型冠状病毒）可能透过飞沫而扩散。）<br />
用法：当名词时，意思是“抹酱”，例如：乳酪抹酱（cheese spread）、巧克力抹酱（chocolate spread）；但是如果要说水果类型抹酱，则会用&#8221;jam&#8221;这个字，例如：草莓酱（strawberry jam）、手作果酱（homemade jam）。</p>
<p>● blame，动词/ble?m/责怪<br />
自然发音：BLAME<br />
英解：to say someone did something wrong<br />
例句：Don&#8217;t blame me! It is not my fault.（不要怪我，又不是我的错。）<br />
用法：怪罪“谁”，介词用&#8221;on&#8221;，例如：John blames his low IQ on his parents.（约翰将他的低智商归咎于父母亲。）<br />
怪罪“什么／事情”，介词用&#8221;for&#8221;，例如：John blames his parents for his low IQ.（约翰将他的低智商归咎于父母亲。）</p>
<p>● Democrats，名词/?dem.?.kr?t/美国民主党人士<br />
自然发音：DE-mo-crats<br />
英解：in the US, a member or supporter of the Democratic Party<br />
例句：The Democrats demanded a recount but still lost by a few votes.（民主党要求重新计算票数，但仍以几票之差输了。）<br />
用法：简写常用&#8221;Dem(s)&#8221;。台湾政党“民主进步党”DPP（Democratic Progressive Party，现任执政党）。</p>
<p>● fault，名词/fɑ?lt/过错<br />
自然发音：FAULT<br />
英解：a mistake, especially something for which you are to blame<br />
例句：Stop accusing me. It wasn&#8217;t my fault.（别再指责我了，又不是我的错。）<br />
用法：另一常用意思为“故障”，例如：The fire was caused by an electrical fault.（火灾是由电线走火引起。）也就是俗称的&#8221;electrical fire&#8221;。</p>
<p>● do a great job of，动词词组，在&#8230;表现出色<br />
自然发音：do-a-GREAT-job-of<br />
英解：to do something very well<br />
例句：You&#8217;ve done a fantastic job of decorating the room.（你把房间布置得非常漂亮。）<br />
用法：另外，形容做着“艰苦又无聊的工作”时，我们可以说&#8221;do the donkey work&#8221;（做着驴子般的工作）。例如：Why should I do all the donkey work?（为何我要做所有这些枯燥乏味的工作？）</p>
<p>● include，动词/?n?klu?d/包含<br />
自然发音：in-CLU-de<br />
英解：to contain something as a part of something else<br />
例句：The price includes tax and service.（价格已包括税和服务费。）<br />
用法：相反地，如果该费用或账单“不含”税或服务费，则可用&#8221;exclude&#8221;，例如：The price （bill） excludes tax and service.<br />
下次出国购物结账前，记得先问清楚：Is the price tax included? ／ Is the price including tax?（这个价钱含税吗？）才不至于误判价格，以为捡到便宜，却大失血！</p>
<p>● certain，指示代名词/?s??(r)t(?)n/某些<br />
自然发音：CER-tain<br />
英解：particular but not named or described<br />
例句：Certain candidates of the party are good indeed.（该政党的某些候选人确实很好。）<br />
用法：口语上我们常说：“某种程度&#8230;”，英文可以说：&#8221;to a certain degree&#8221;</p>
<p>● oppose，动词/??po?z/反对<br />
自然发音：o-PPO-se<br />
英解：to disagree with something or someone<br />
例句：The new testing system has been vigorously opposed by most teachers.（新的测验方式遭到大多数老师的强烈反对。）<br />
用法：也可以用词组方式表达“反对”之意，例如：She&#8217;s opposed to any changes to the current legislation.（她反对改变现有的立法制度。）</p>
<p>● turn out，动词，（出乎意料的）结果是&#8230;<br />
自然发音：TURN-out<br />
英解：to have a particular result, especially an unexpected one<br />
例句：She thought she was pregnant, but it turned out to be a false alarm.<br />
（她以为自己怀孕了，但事实证明虚惊一场。）<br />
用法：额外一提，&#8221;false alarm&#8221;（直译：错误的闹钟／警报），可以解释为“摆一场乌龙”，例如：Someone called to say a fire, but it turned out to be a false alarm.（有人打电话说某处发生火灾，但结果却是乌龙警报。）</p>
<p>● incompetent，形容词/?n?kɑ?m.p?.t??nt/无能力的（不能胜任的）<br />
自然发音：in-COM-pe-tent<br />
英解：not having the ability to do something as it should be done<br />
例句：He has described the government as corrupt and incompetent.（他批评政府的腐败与无能。）<br />
用法：competent“能干的、称职的”，例如：They are a very competent group of people, but they lack flexibility and originality.（他们是一组非常有能力的人，但他们缺乏弹性和创新。）</p>
