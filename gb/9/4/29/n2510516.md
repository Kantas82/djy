<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>看新闻，学英语(160)科学奇想</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p><--ads--><B>Daydreaming Science</B><br />科学奇想</p> <p>本则英语影音新闻及出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻 <ahref= >http://english.ntdtv.com/ntdtv_en/ns_europe/2009-04-06/343344023291.md#1<br /></a>（可线上观看）</p> <p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1.<B>behaviour</B>： n. 变化状态<br />2.<B>dynamics</B>： n. 动态<br />3.<B>cumulus</B> ：n. 积云<br />4.<B>cirrus</B> ：n. 卷云<br />5.<B>baffle</B> ：v. 困扰<br />6.<B>meteorologist</B> ：n. 气象学家<br />7.<B>up to now</B>： ph. 到目前为止</p> <p>STORY:<br />Dutch scientists are saying that the <B>behaviour</B> of low clouds is the big mystery in climate predictions.<br />报导内容：<br />荷兰科学家表示，低云的变化状态，是气象预测的一个大谜团。</p> <p>Clouds baffle scientists who say greater understanding of their <B>dynamics</B> is needed for accurate climate predictions.<br />这些云困扰科学家，他们表示，必须更了解云的动态，以便精准的预测气象。</p> <p>From <B>cumulus</B> to <B>cirrus</B>, clouds have fascinated painters and poets. But they <B>baffle</B> scientists who say a greater understanding of them is needed for accurate climate predictions. <br />无论是积云还是卷云，都强烈地吸引画家及诗人们，但是也困扰科学家，必须更了解云的动态，科学家才能精准的预测气象。</p> <p><B>Meteorologists</B> in the Netherlands have set up a virtual cloud laboratory to study clouds.<br />荷兰的气象学家，设立了一个虚拟云的实验室来研究它。</p> <p>[Harm Jonker, professor at TU Delft]: &#8220;<B>Up to now</B> we have no idea if in a warmer climate there will be more clouds or less clouds and this will have a huge impact.&#8221; <br />[哈姆琼克，台夫特科技大学教授]：<br />“时至今日，我们仍不知道如果在一个比较温暖的天气，到底会有更多云还是更少云，而这将有很大的影响。”</p> <p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1. <B>reflect</B>： v. 反射<br />2.<B>vapour</B>： n. 蒸气<br />3.<B>debate </B>：v. 争论; 辩论<br />4.<B>greenhouse</B>： n. 温室<br />5.<B>intergovernmental</B>： adj. 政府与政府间的<br />6.<B>unknown</B>： n. 未知的事物</p> <p>Jonker and other climate scientists at Delft focus their laboratory studies on the lower &#8216;fairweather&#8217; clouds such as cumulus clouds, which <B>reflect</B> sunlight away from the earth and therefore have a cooling effect. <br />琼克和其它在台夫特的天气科学家，在他们的实验室专门研究低云层的‘好天气’，例如积云，因它可以反射太阳光而有冷却的效果。</p> <p>They are also investigating theories that there will be fewer fairweather clouds in warmer temperatures, because warm air can hold more water <B>vapour</B> than cold air. They also think that with fewer clouds around to reflect sunlight, global warming could accelerate. <br />他们也探讨较热天时，好天气的云将会较少等各种理论，因为暖空气比冷空气含更多水蒸气。他们也在思考，如果愈来愈少的云来反射太阳光，是否会加速全球暖化。 </p> <p>Scientists at Delft said one of the problems with <B>debating</B> about climate change is that attention is focused mainly on the polluting effect of <B>greenhouse</B> gases.<br />台夫特的科学家表示，关于气候变迁争论中的一个议题，就是目前注意力都放在温室气体所造成的污染效应。</p> <p>[Harm Jonker, professor at TU Delft]: &#8220;Of course it&#8217;s very good there is a huge focus on greenhouse gases and what&#8217;s the result of them, but that&#8217;s only one side of the story. The other side of the story is how will the Earth respond to those changes and in that sense clouds play a major role.&#8221;<br />[哈姆琼克，台夫特科技大学教授]：<br />“当然，把焦点放在温室气体及其造成的结果，这是很好的，但是这只是整件事情的一个面向。另一个面向是，地球对这些改变将会如何回应，就此而言，云在这里扮演了一个主要角色。”</p> <p>The U.N. <B>Intergovernmental</B> Panel on Climate Change said in its most recent report that cloud formation was one of the biggest <B>unknowns</B> in climate predictions.<br />联合国的政府间气候变化专门委员会表示，在它们最近的报告中，云的形成是气象预测中最大的未知数之一。<br />@*</p> <p><font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
