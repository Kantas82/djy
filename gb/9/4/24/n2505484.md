<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>看新闻，学英语(159)中国孤独的孩子</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p><B>China&#8217;s Lonely Children</B><br />中国孤独的孩子</p> <p>本则英语影音新闻及出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻 <ahref= >http://english.ntdtv.com/ntdtv_en/ns_china/2009-04-02/395324783811.md#1<br /></a>（可线上观看） </p> <p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1. <B>autism</B>： n. 自闭症<br />2.<B>misfit</B>： n. 不适应环境的人<br />3.<B>playground </B>：n. 游乐场<br />4.<B>stool</B>： n. 凳子<br />5.<B>breakthrough </B>：n. 突破<br />6.<B>syndrome</B>： n. 综合症状<br />7.<B>cope</B>： v. 妥善处理<br />8.<B>thin</B>： adj. 薄弱的<br />9.<B>desperate</B>： adj. 绝望的</p> <p><B>Autism</B> affects roughly 67 million people worldwide. But in China, families living with autistic children receive almost no help from the communist regime, and parents struggle to care for their children-children who are seen as social <B>misfits</B>.<br />自闭症在全球影响了大约六千七百万人。然而，在中国拥有自闭症小孩的家庭，却几乎完全没有获得共产政府的帮助。父母得很艰辛地照顾他们的孩子－那些被视为社会适应不良的孩子。</p> <p>A <B>playground</B> race around an old <B>stool</B> might look simple, but for these parents, it&#8217;s a major <B>breakthrough</B>.<br />一个绕着一张张老旧凳子的游乐场比赛，看起来似乎很简单，但对这些父母来说，这是一个重大的突破。</p> <p>&#8220;Stars and Rain&#8221; was the first school in China to help children with &#8220;lonely <B>syndrome</B>,&#8221; what the Chinese call autism.<br />“星和雨”是中国第一个致力于帮助 “自闭症候群” 孩子的学校，也就是中国人所称的自闭症。</p> <p>The school teaches children simple tasks as well as teaches parents ways to <B>cope</B>.<br />这间学校教导孩子简单的工作，同时也教导父母妥善处理孩子的方法。</p> <p>Autism is still relatively unknown here, and the country&#8217;s <B>thin</B> welfare system offers little help.<br />在这里，自闭症仍然相对的不被大多数人所了解，而且这个国家薄弱的福利系统，所提供的帮助极为稀少。</p> <p>Friends and neighbors often don&#8217;t understand, so the parents are <B>desperate</B>, says school founder Tian Huiping.<br />学校创办人唐和平说，朋友以及邻居通常都不理解，所以父母们都感到很绝望。</p> <p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1. <B> disabled</B>： adj. 残废的; 有缺陷的<br />2.<B>firstborn</B>： n. 长子; 长女<br />3.<B>treatment</B>： n. 治疗<br />4.<B>vast</B>： adj. 巨大的<br />5.<B>prominence</B>： n. 凸出; 显着</p> <p>[Tian Huiping, Founder of Stars and Rain]: <br />&#8220;If I have a son and the son marries a wife, then he and his wife will support us when we are older and, if for a family the child is <B>disabled</B>, then the future is very dark.&#8221;<br />[唐和平，星和雨创办人]：<br />“如果我有一个儿子，这个儿子娶了一个妻子，那么当我们变老时，他和他的妻子将会扶养我们。如果一个家庭中的小孩有缺陷，那么未来将是非常黑暗的。”</p> <p>China&#8217;s one-child policy allows parents of disabled children to try for another, but many, like Gao Shufeng&#8217;s daughter, choose not to and focus on their <B>firstborn</B>.<br />中国的一胎化政策允许有缺陷的小孩的父母再生一个，但是，有很多像高树峰的女儿，选择不再生了，并且专心照顾他们头一个孩子。</p> <p>[Gao Shufeng, Grandmother of Autistic Child]: <br />&#8220;There is no way through it, sometimes they urge the mother to give up the child and try for another. But his mother won&#8217;t do it, she won&#8217;t give up. She says that if there is advanced <B>treatment</B> in foreign countries then there must be a way here.&#8221;<br />[高树峰，自闭症小孩的祖母]：<br />“有时候真没办法，有时候他们叫母亲放弃这个孩子，再试另一个。但是他的妈妈不愿意这么做，她不愿意放弃。她说如果在其它国家有更先进的治疗方法，那么这儿也应该要有啊。”</p> <p>Data suggests over 2 million people of China&#8217;s <B>vast</B> population of 1.3 billion may have autism, meaning the issue will likely grow in <B>prominence</B> in coming years.<br />资料显示，中国广大的十三亿人口中，超过两百万人可能拥有自闭症，这表示在来年这项议题可能日益显着。<br />@* </p> <p><font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
