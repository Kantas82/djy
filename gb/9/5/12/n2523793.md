<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>看新闻，学英语(163)：中国人发明“步行椅”</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p><B>Chinese Invents “Walking Chair” </B><br />中国人发明“步行椅”</p> <p>本则英语影音新闻及出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻 <ahref= >http://english.ntdtv.com/ntdtv_en/ns_life/2009-04-02/288463386813.md#1 </a>（可线上观看）”</p> <p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1. <B>retiree</B>: n. 退休者<br />2. <B>hectic</B>: adj. 忙碌的<br />3. <B>buzz</B>: n. 嗡嗡声<br />4. <B>debut</B>: n.首演 <br />5. <B>disabled people</B>: n. 残障人士<br />6. staircases: n. 楼梯</p> <p>Like many people, 65-year-old Beijing resident Li Rongbiao was looking forward to peace and quiet after he retired from his job a few years ago. But after an accident in his family, this <B>retiree</B> now finds his life more <B>hectic</B> than ever.<br />住在北京现年65岁的李荣标（音译）和多数人一样，自前几年退休后便开始追寻平和宁静的生活。但自从家中遭逢变故，他的退休生活面临前所末有的繁忙</p> <p>Li is the inventor of what he calls &#8220;the walking chair,&#8221; which has created a <B>buzz</B> after making its <B>debut</B> at China &#8216;s largest expo for <B>disabled people</B> two months ago.<br />李荣标是他称之为“步行椅”的发明者，两个月前这项发明在中国最大的身障者博览会亮相时造成不小骚动。</p> <p>[Li Rongbiao, Walking Chair Inventor]:<br />&#8220;One time, my wife and I were taking a walk. But she fell and broke her leg. It was so difficult for me to get her upstairs. That&#8217;s why I decided I should invent a &#8216;transportation tool&#8217; for <B>staircases</B>.&#8221;<br />李荣标说：“有次我太太和我在散步时，她跌倒摔断了腿。从此我要把她扶上楼是很困难的，所以我决定发明一个上下楼的‘交通工具’。”</p> <p><p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />7. <B>crumbling</B>: adj. 破烂的 <br />8. <B>spare</B>: n. 零件<br />9. <B>wheelchair</B>: n. 轮椅<br />10. <B>appeal</B>: n. 吸引力<br />11.<B>innovation</B>: n. 创新<br />12.<B>pension</B>: n. 养老金</p> <p>Orders are now flying in and Li&#8217;s <B>crumbling</B> workshop in the suburbs of Beijing receives more visitors from across the country.<br />除了订单涌入，李荣标在北京郊区残破的工作室，现在得接待更多自来全国各地的访客。</p> <p>The &#8220;walking chair&#8221; is an electric chair made out of over 1,000 <B>spares</B>. It can take people up and down stairs and also works as a <B>wheelchair</B> on flat ground.<br />“步行椅”是由一千多个零件组成的电动椅，不只可以带人上下楼，在平地也可做一般轮椅使用。</p> <p>Li says part of the <B>appeal</B> of his chair is that it costs much less than similar products in Germany or Japan . It sells for about article content begin -->,500.<br />李荣标说“步行椅”的吸引力之一，是价格远比同类的德、日产品便宜，约美金一千五百元。</p> <p>To fund the research and the various drafts of his chair, Li sold his house in Beijing and took out his life&#8217;s savings and <B>pension</B>. He then spent two years perfecting his <B>innovation</B> inside a simple workshop some 28 kilometers outside the city.<br />为了筹措“步行椅”各种不同形式的研究基金，李荣标卖了北京的房子，还领出毕生积蓄及养老金。他接着花了两年时间，在城外28公里的简陋工作室内，将他这项创新修饰的更加完美。</p> <p>Li always kept the door of his workshop closed so he could work in peace just a little to peaceful for authorities who thought he could be making explosives.<br /> 为求宁静，李荣标的工作室总是大门深锁，为此当局还怀疑他可能在制造炸弹。</p> <p>After reassuring police that his inventions were to help, Li was able to hire seven workers from the area.<br />李荣标向警察解释他的发明对社会有益后，总算能在当地雇用七名员工。</p> <p>It takes the workers two and a half days to finish one chair because they are all made by hand.<br />因为是全手工制作，工人要花两天半的时间才能完成一辆“步行椅”。</p> <p>So far, Li has sold 30 chairs, but has orders for over 300 waiting to be filled.<br />到目前为止，李荣标卖了三十辆“步行椅”，然而还有三百多张订单在等着他。<br />@*</p> <p> <p><font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
