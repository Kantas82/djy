<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>看新闻学英语(180)：壮观的日蚀</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p><B>Solar Eclipse Spectacular Impresses Asia</B><br />壮观日蚀令亚洲印象深刻</p>

<p>本则影音新闻出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻</p>
<p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1. <B> dip</B>: n. 浸泡<br />2.<B>Ganges</B>: n.恒河<br />3.<B>emerge</B>: v. 浮现<br />4. <B> enthusiast</B>: n. 热情人士<br />5. <B> goggles</B>: n. 护目镜 <br />6.<B>glimpse</B>: n. 瞥见<br />7. <B> phenomenon</B>: n. 现象<br />8.<B>miraculous</B>: adj. 奇迹的<br />9.<B>dampen</B>: vt. 使沮丧 <br />10.<B>disappointing</B>:adj. 令人失望的<br />11. <B>occur</B>: vi.发生<br />12. <B>landfall</B>: n.着陆</p>
<p>It lasted six minutes and 39 seconds – not usually a long time but it’s the longest solar eclipse witnessed for 100 years.<br />持续6分39秒－一般这不是一段很长的时间，但却是100年来最长的日全蚀。</p>
<p>Beginning at dawn in India, tens of thousands gathered for a <B>dip</B> in the <B>Ganges</B> to pray to the sun, as it <B>emerged</B> from behind the moon. Hundreds of <B>enthusiasts</B> wearing <B>goggles</B> catch a <B>glimpse</B> of the <B>phenomenon</B>.<br />从在印度的黎明开始，成千上万的人聚集在恒河中浸泡，当太阳从月亮之后浮现出来时，向太阳祈祷。数百位热情人士戴着护目镜捕捉日食现象的惊鸿一瞥。</p>
<p>In China, emotions ran high among those gathered along a river bank in Chongqing city.<br />在中国，聚集在重庆市河岸的人们，情绪非常的高昂。</p>
<p>[Jian Tian, Chongqing Resident]:<br />“It is a <B>miraculous</B> experience. The moment the total eclipse occurred, I was struck emotionally. I felt like I couldn&#8217;t look through my glasses and had to witness this magnificent natural phenomenon with the naked eye.”<br />一位重庆居民说：“这是个奇迹般的经验，日全食发生的这一刻，我在情绪上非常的受到震撼，我感到不能透过眼镜来看，而须用我的肉眼来目睹这伟大的自然现象。”</p>
<p>In Shanghai, dark clouds left the sky overcast, <B>dampening</B> the mood for those all prepared to view the eclipse. But many braved the rain to experience the sky turning from light to pitch darkness in minutes.<br />在上海，乌云将整个天空遮蔽了，抑制了所有准备好看日蚀的人们的情绪。但是有许多人不畏雨势，去经验天空在几分钟内从光亮变成黑暗。</p>
<p>[Yang Xingyu, Shanghai Resident]:<br />&#8220;Of course it’s <B>disappointing</B> to not have seen an eclipse that only happens once every 300 years. Even though we didn’t see the sun, we experienced darkness during the day.&#8221;<br />一位上海居民说：“当然没有看到300年才会发生一次的日蚀，是很令人失望的。但即使我们没有看到太阳，我们经验了在白天中的黑暗。”</p>
<p>Those in the southern islands of Japan had better luck with the weather. As the sun slowly is covered by the moon, excitement grew.<br />那些聚集在日本南部岛屿的人们运气最好，拥有最好的观测天侯。当太阳慢慢的被月亮覆盖的时候，兴奋之情油然上升。</p>
<p>[Tatsuya Miyakoshi, Witness]:<br />“It almost made me cry. My legs were shaking.&#8221;<br />一位目击者说：“日全食几乎让我哭泣，我的腿一直在颤抖着。”</p>
<p>The next total eclipse will <B>occur</B> in July 2010, making <B>landfall</B> only on the Cook and Easter Islands in the South Pacific Ocean.<br />下一次日全食将会在2010年7月发生，但只有在南太平洋的库克岛与复活岛上才能够看到。◇</p>
