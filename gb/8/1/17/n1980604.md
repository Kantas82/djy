<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>流行美语 第260课</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p>【大纪元1月17日讯】<center><ahref=http://www.freexinwen.com/chinese/eng/pa/download.asp?file=/chinese/s_pa/pa260.mp3 >(MP3下载)</a></center>	</p> <p>Larry和李华去朋友Frank家参加新年派对。李华今天要学两个常用语，dead和happening.</p> <p>LL: Gosh, there is hardly any one here, Li Hua. This party is really dead!</p> <p>LH: Larry. 这里每个人明明都活得好好的，你为什么说the party is dead呢？</p> <p>LL: Well, because there aren&#8217;t very many people here, there isn&#8217;t much conversation and laughter. When a party isn&#8217;t lively, we say it is DEAD. And this party is dead.</p> <p>LH: 噢，你是说这个party不热闹，了无生气啊。现在时间还早，也许一会儿就热闹起来了。</p> <p>LL: I hope so. Speaking of places being dead today, I went into work for a little bit to get some things from my desk. There was no one in the office &#8211; it was completely dead.</p> <p>LH: 那当然了，今天是除夕，没有什么人会去办公室的。</p> <p>LL: I guess the college campus must be dead now, too.</p> <p>LH: 不光是今天，期终考试结束后，学生们都回家过年了。The campus has been dead for two weeks.</p> <p>LL: So, Li Hua, do you mind being on campus when it is dead like it is now?</p> <p>LH: 确实有点孤单，不过人少也有人少的好处。比方说，图书馆里就特别安静。</p> <p>LL: Oh, I bet the library is completely dead! I mean, who would be studying over the break?</p> <p>LH: 我啊！不过你说的没错，校园里确实没有什么人，可是停车特别方便。</p> <p>LL: I know what you mean about the convenience. This year I made sure to do my Christmas shopping at a time when the shopping mall was really dead.</p> <p>LH: 圣诞购物季节，商店里到处都是人。I cannot imagine a mall being dead during the holidays.</p> <p>LL: I went early on Tuesday and Wednesday morning. Since most people work during those times, it was pretty dead at the mall.</p> <p>LH: 这倒是个好办法，可以避开购物的人群，不用跟别人挤来挤去的。</p> <p>LL: That&#8217;s right, Li Hua. Hey, look, the party isn&#8217;t so dead any more! Some more people have shown up, and the music has started.</p> <p>LH: The party isn&#8217;t dead at all. 你想去跳舞吗？</p> <p>******</p> <p>LL: Sure, I&#8217;d love to dance, Li Hua. Now the party is really happening!</p> <p>LH: 如果我没猜错得话，Happening是dead的反义词吧？</p> <p>LL: You are right, Li Hua. It is the opposite of a &#8220;dead&#8221; party. A happening party is one that is lively and exciting &#8211; and where lots of different things are happening.</p> <p>LH: 是啊，这个party好像一下子就热闹起来了。</p> <p>LL: Frank has done a good job. It is because of parties like this that I love the holidays. Christmas and New Year&#8217;s are such happening times of the year.</p> <p>LH: 就是啊，新年圣诞确实热闹，大家都跟亲朋好友在一起，不过我也很喜欢春天和夏天。</p> <p>LL: Why do you like spring and summer, Li Hua?</p> <p>LH: 放春假和暑假我可以到外地去，visit some more happening places.</p> <p>LL: I can&#8217;t imagine a more happening place than New York City. I mean here in New York there is always something going on!</p> <p>LH: 纽约确实很热闹，可是美国还有其他热闹的地方啊。</p> <p>LL: I suppose that is true. I&#8217;ve heard San Francisco has a very happening art scene.</p> <p>LH: 我就很想去旧金山看看。还有，我还特别喜欢美国的布鲁斯音乐，所以很想到孟菲斯去。</p> <p>LL: I don&#8217;t know, Li Hua. I visited Memphis once, and I didn&#8217;t think it was a very happening place. But then again, I am not a fan of Blues music.</p> <p>LH: 猫王的雅园就在孟菲斯。我一直想去看看。我想，他当年在雅园一定开过很多热闹的派对。</p> <p>LL: Elvis was famous for his wild parties. Look, the party is really happening now.</p> <p>LH: Come on, Larry. 我们去跳舞吧，这么热闹的party, 我可不想就站在这儿。Let&#8217;s go have some fun!</p> <p>LL: Okay, Li Hua! Now this is my idea of a happening time!</p> <p>今天李华学到了两个常用语。一个是dead, 指了无生气。另一个是happening,指非常热闹。<br /><font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
