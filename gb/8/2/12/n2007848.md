<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>流行美语 第264课</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p>【大纪元2月12日讯】<center><ahref=http://www.freexinwen.com/chinese/eng/pa/download.asp?file=/chinese/s_pa/pa264.mp3 >(MP3下载)</a></center>	</p> <p>李华周末来看Larry。李华今天要学两个常用语，goof around和go halvsies.</p> <p>LL: It sure is nice to see you, Li Hua. But, honestly I&#8217;ve got so many things I have to do right now. I&#8217;ve just goofed around all weekend and haven&#8217;t gotten anything done.</p> <p>LH: You goofed around? 这是什么意思啊？</p> <p>LL: To &#8220;goof around&#8221; means to not do the things you are supposed to do. And I really didn&#8217;t do what I was supposed to this weekend.</p> <p>LH: 噢，原来goof around是游手好闲，不务正业的意思。那你有什么该做的事情没有做呢？</p> <p>LL: I was supposed to do my laundry, dust and vacuum my apartment, mop my kitchen floor, and clean my bathroom. But because I goofed around, I didn&#8217;t do any of the cleaning I needed to do.</p> <p>LH: 洗衣服、还要打扫房间、擦地板，这么多事啊！那你这两天都干了些什么呢？</p> <p>LL: Instead of doing my housework, I watched movies on TV, talked to friends on the phone, and played computer games.</p> <p>LH: You really did goof around. 又是看电影，又是打游戏的。难道你父母没有告诉过你，只有做完正事以后才能玩吗？</p> <p>LL: They certainly did, Li Hua. But after a full week of work, I really didn&#8217;t feel like doing my house cleaning. In fact, I didn&#8217;t want to do much else besides goof around.</p> <p>LH: 这我能理解，大家周末都想好好放松一下，不过也不能整个周末都这样无所事事啊。</p> <p>LL: I know you are right, Li Hua. The weekend is really the only time I have to make sure my apartment is clean and that I have clean clothes.</p> <p>LH: 现在可倒好，已经星期天晚上了，你明天上班有干净衣服穿吗！</p> <p>LL: Well, then I&#8217;ve goofed around enough! I really need to do my laundry, Li Hua. So if you don&#8217;t mind, I am going to get to work.</p> <p>LH: 我本来想和你出去看场电影，喝杯咖啡的，现在全泡汤了。</p> <p>LL: Yeah, I am sorry, Li Hua. You shouldn&#8217;t have to go home, because I goofed around.</p> <p>LH: 这样好了，我们商量商量，我可以帮你一起打扫房间。</p> <p>LL: And then afterwards, we could still go for a cup of coffee.</p> <p>*****</p> <p>LL: I have a proposal for you, Li Hua. How about we go halvsies on my housework?</p> <p>LH: Go halvsies，那又是什么意思啊？</p> <p>LL: To &#8220;go halvsies&#8221; is to split something in half. In this case, I was hoping you could do half of my housework.</p> <p>LH: 哎谁让你是我朋友呢！我愿意帮你干一半的家务。可是我们怎么分担呢？</p> <p>LL: We can go halvsies by me doing the laundry and cleaning the bathroom, and you mopping the kitchen and doing the dusting and vacuuming.</p> <p>LH: 等等，我得写下来，免得你反悔。你说你负责洗衣服和打扫厕所，我负责拖厨房的地板和吸尘。这么多事情，干完肯定累死了。那我们到时候可以出去吃东西。</p> <p>LL: Well, rather than go out to eat, let&#8217;s go halvsies on ordering a pizza for delivery.</p> <p>LH: 你是说咱俩各出一半的钱，点个比萨回来吃？好啊，我同意！</p> <p>LL: Great! If we go halvsies on ordering a pizza for delivery right to the apartment, we will still have a lot of time. I&#8217;ll call the pizza place. How many pizzas should I order?</p> <p>LH: 我不是特别饿，点个大号的，就应该够咱俩吃的了。</p> <p>LL: Okay, then one pizza it is. I&#8217;ll also order a bottle of soda, and we can go halvsies on that, too.</p> <p>LH: 汽水？好啊，不过都是你的，我可不想喝。</p> <p>LL: How about dessert? We can go halvsies on some cookies or a pie?</p> <p>LH: 我有个更好的主意。与其吃饼干，不如我们干完活一起去吃冰激凌吧，也算是慰劳一下自己。</p> <p>LL: Good idea! After we finish working, we can go halvsies on some ice cream.</p> <p>LH: 我可没说要跟你分，整个冰激凌都是我一个人的！</p> <p>今天李华学到了两个常用语。一个是goof around, 是消磨时间，不务正业的意思。另一个是go halvsies, 是一人一半的意思。<font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
