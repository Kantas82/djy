<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>流行美语 第266课</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p>【大纪元2月29日讯】<center><ahref=http://www.freexinwen.com/chinese/eng/pa/download.asp?file=/chinese/s_pa/pa266.mp3 >(MP3下载)</a></center>	</p> <p>美式足球超级杯那天，Larry要在家开party, 李华帮他做准备。李华今天要学两个常用语，munchies和spread.</p> <p>LL: I&#8217;m really excited about having a Super Bowl party, Li Hua. I just went to the store and bought lots of munchies, so we will have plenty to eat while we watch the football game.</p> <p>LH: Munchies? 让我看看？(翻塑料袋的声音)。薯条、花生、开心果，I don&#8217;t see anything called &#8220;munchies.&#8221;</p> <p>LL: Munchies is a general word for snacks or things to &#8220;munch&#8221; on, Li Hua. When watching a football game, I love to eat munchies like chips and pretzels.</p> <p>LH: 噢，原来munchies是零食的意思！有不咸的吗？</p> <p>LL: Yes, Li Hua, I do. In the refrigerator, I have some other munchies that I&#8217;ve prepared. I also made a vegetable tray and some sour cream dip.</p> <p>LH: Wow, 你准备了这么多东西！ 看超级杯原来这么重要啊！</p> <p>LL: Watching the Super Bowl is something many Americans look forward to all year. And part of the fun is inviting friends over to watch the game and share some munchies.</p> <p>LH: 这跟中国球迷看足球世界杯差不多。他们也是围着电视机，边吃边看，热闹极了。</p> <p>LL: Every body loves munchies. Speaking of munchies, are you hungry, Li Hua?</p> <p>LH: 我确实有点儿饿了，不过我不想把这些零食打开，我们还是等客人都到了再说吧！</p> <p>LL: Oh, don&#8217;t worry about it, Li Hua. There are plenty of munchies. Go ahead and open what you like.</p> <p>LH: 那好，我先吃点花生，我最爱吃花生了。</p> <p>LL: I&#8217;ve got a bad case of the munchies, too, Li Hua. But instead of peanuts, I think I&#8217;ll munch on some potato chips.</p> <p>LH: A *case* of the munchies? 你拿的明明是一带零食啊！</p> <p>LL: When I said I have a case of the munchies, I meant I was really hungry for snacks.</p> <p>LH: Well, I guess I have a case of the munchies, too, Larry. 这些花生真是太好吃了。</p> <p>LL: The trouble with munchies is that once you start eating them &#8211; it is hard to stop.</p> <p>LH: 那我们还是忍忍吧，可别把这些零食都吃完了，否则一会儿看球赛的时候就没的吃了。</p> <p>******</p> <p>LL: Thanks for helping me put out the food, Li Hua. I hope this is enough of a spread for the Super Bowl party.</p> <p>LH: Enough of a spread? 这又是什么意思啊？你是说足够大家吃的吧？</p> <p>LL: A spread does refer to food, Li Hua &#8211; and specifically food set out on a table for a party. And the goal is to have lots of food!</p> <p>LH: 你准备的可真够丰盛的，That&#8217;s quite a spread, Larry. 你看，有薯条、花生、开心果、蔬菜、饮料、啤酒，真是应有尽有。</p> <p>LL: Well, when one puts together a spread, Li Hua, you not only want there to be lots of food, but you also want the spread to look good.</p> <p>LH: 所以说，spread不光是要准备充足的食物，还要摆放得整齐好看，让人看了赏心悦目？</p> <p>LL: That&#8217;s right. Like for this spread, I put the snacks in nice bowls, arranged the vegetables so they look attractive, and got these festive football napkins and cups.</p> <p>LH: 太棒了，Larry。我知道，你这一桌子食物，从盘子到纸杯，到餐巾纸，都跟橄榄球有关，最适合超级杯的party了.</p> <p>LL: Thanks, Li Hua. I&#8217;m glad you like it.</p> <p>LH: Larry, 你准备这一桌子东西要花很多钱吗？</p> <p>LL: This spread wasn&#8217;t very expensive. But I have been to parties where the hosts clearly spent a lot of money on gourmet foods and expensive wines.</p> <p>LH: 我还记得去年到市中心的一家大饭店去参加春节联欢活动。They had quite a spread!</p> <p>LL: I bet it was some spread. You know how much I like Chinese food.</p> <p>LH: 那明年咱们一起去，你可以亲眼看看！</p> <p>LL: While the spread I put together isn&#8217;t as fancy as that, I just hope my friends will enjoy it.</p> <p>今天李华学到了两个常用语。一个是munchies, 是零食的意思。另一个是spread, 指丰盛的食物。<font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
