<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>许其正：黄山掠影</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p><b>Bird’s-Eye View of Mt. Huangshan</b> 　Hsu Chicheng</p> <p> 莲花峰</p> <p>四周小峰簇拥着<br />让主峰高耸突出<br />这莲花峰正如<br />新莲初开，冲霄怒放</p> <p>登临其上，晴时<br />东望天目，西瞻匡庐，北窥<br />九华与长江，雨后出晴<br />再将云海和痴念尽收眼底<br />哦！且独坐这云霄<br />静观世态万变吧！</p> <p> Lotus Peak</p> <p>Set off by low peaks all around<br />Lotus Peak reaches to the clouds<br />Just like lotus<br />In full blossoms</p> <p>Reaching its top in a sunny day<br />Watching Mt. Tianmu to the east<br />Watching Mt. Lusan to the west<br />Watching to Mt. Jiuhua and Yangtze River to the north<br />After gloom comes brightness, sea of clouds and chimerical idea come into view<br />O, let me sit alone on the clouds watching all manifestation of universe!</p> <p> 始信峰</p> <p>漫说黄山风景奇绝<br />有名峰高耸<br />幽谷悬崖<br />秀水瀑布<br />巧石争妍<br />奇松林立<br />云蒸霞蔚<br />只有跟随黄习远<br />亲来践履，一窥其奥<br />始信!<br />注：相传黄习远自云谷寺游至此峰，如入画境，似幻而真，方信黄山风景奇绝，题名“始信”，此峰因以得名。</p> <p> Shixin Peak</p> <p>The scenery of Mt. Huangshan is famous for its magnificence<br />High peaks towering into the sky<br />Deep valleys and overhanging cliffs<br />Beautiful waters and waterfalls<br />Jagged rocks of grotesque shapes<br />Ancient pines of grotesque shapes and appearances<br />All look magnificent<br />Only when you make a tour of Mt. Huangshan<br />Following Huang Xiyuan<br />You will be wholly convinced by its unchanting beautiy!<br />Note:Tradition has it that a person named Huang Xiyuan in the Ming Dynasty, once reached this peak from Yungu Temple, he felt as if entering a beautiful dreamland, so he named this peak Shixin Peak.</p> <p> 光明顶</p> <p>旁无依附<br />兀立<br />如一覆钵</p> <p>高旷开阔<br />日光照射<br />光明呀光明</p> <p>朝奉吧!<br />黄山六峰，以及<br />更远的天目、庐山……</p> <p> Bright Summit</p> <p>Standing erect<br />All alone<br />Like an alms bowl place upside down</p> <p>High, spacious and wide<br />Bathed in sunlight<br />So bright, so bright</p> <p>Swear fealty to it!<br />The other six peaks of Mt. Huangshan<br />Mt. Tianmu and Mt. Liushan in the distance…</p> <p> 猴子观海</p> <p>蹲立峰顶<br />石猴张开手掌放在眉头上<br />眺向前方</p> <p>身子左右微晃<br />努力搜寻着<br />他在眺望、搜寻什么？</p> <p>前方呀<br />阴时，云海<br />晴时，太平</p> <p>注：猴子观海又名猴观太平。太平指太平县，为安徽黄山市所属黄山区。</p> <p>Monkey Watching the Sea</p> <p>Squatting on the summit<br />A monkey-shaped rock is looking in distance<br />With one hand across its forehead</p> <p>With its body shaking gently<br />It is trying to seek<br />What is he watching and seeking?</p> <p>In the distance<br />Appears the sea of clouds when it is gloomy<br />Peace and franquillity when it is sunny</p> <p>Note: “Monkey Watching the Sea” alias “Monkey Enjoying Peace and Tranquillity”. Here “Peace and Tranquillity” refers in meaning to the former Taiping County – now Taiping District of Huangshan City, Anhui prov.</p> <p> 仙人下棋</p> <p>身穿道袍，头挽发髻的仙人<br />成排在那里干什么？<br />闲聊？散步？</p> <p>唯独两人对坐<br />以平整的树冠当棋枰<br />潜心下棋<br />车马奔驰……<br />烟雾弥漫……<br />炮声隆隆……</p> <p>观棋的丞相是裁判？<br />背宝的仙人是给奖者？<br />棋枰的风云会伤及他们吗？</p> <p> Immortals Playing Chess</p> <p>What are they doing, the row of immortals<br />In Taoist robes and with their hair worn in a bun?<br />Are they chatting or walking?</p> <p>Only two of them are sitting face to face<br />Playing chess attentively<br />With a flat-roofed crown as the chessboard<br />Carriages and horses running…<br />Smoken-laden…<br />Rumble of booming guns…</p> <p>Is the Chief Minister watching a chess game the judge?<br />Is the immortal carrying treasure on his back the award-giver?<br />Will they be injured by the wind of chessboard?</p> <p> 关公挡曹</p> <p>黄山这本大书里<br />包罗万象，无奇不有<br />记载“关公挡曹”的历史掌故<br />其实不足奇<br />读来饶富趣味</p> <p>史页历历如昨<br />画面栩栩如生<br />跨马的关公，何须理会<br />曹操的卑躬屈膝？<br />久藏的恩恩怨怨<br />何故还翻出来晾晒？</p> <p> Guan Yu Standing in Cao Cao’s Way</p> <p>Mt. Huangshan, a great historical records<br />All-inclusive and with many wonderful views<br />The historical anecdotes about “Guan Yu Standing in Cao cao’s Way”<br />Has nothing to be surprised at<br />Yet it affords the greatest delight in reading</p> <p>The historical events come clearly into view<br />And the tableaus are lifelike<br />Actually, there was no need for Guan Yu<br />To have a pity on Cao Cao bowing before his horse<br />Why the old scores<br />Were brought to aired again?</p> <p> 松谷</p> <p>岩壁陡峭，耸向苍穹<br />太阳光难以寻隙而入<br />这里有满山满谷的<br />溪、瀑、潭、池……<br />丛生万树<br />郁绿青苔</p> <p>沿谷登山<br />经…“仙人铺路”<br />再走“十八道弯”<br />到达北海<br />心中深深根植着<br />无数奇松</p> <p> Pines Ravine</p> <p>Cliffs rise steeply towering into the clouds<br />Sunlight is hard to find a crevice to pass through<br />Streams, waterfalls, pond and pools<br />Appear everywhere thickly<br />Lichen, lush and green</p> <p>Climbing along the valley<br />Through the “Fairy-build Road”<br />Then the “Eighteen Bends”<br />Reaching “The North Sea” at last<br />Countless exotic pines<br />Taking root in my mind</p> <p>云谷</p> <p>进入云谷<br />穿梭于<br />群峰和溪水之间<br />云飞雾绕之间<br />林、泉、松、石之间<br />便仿佛进入三度空间<br />所有空间美纷纷撞来</p> <p>进入云谷<br />眼见五光十色的美景<br />耳听天籁人音<br />鼻闻草木和泥土的芳馨<br />让微风轻拂<br />让云雾扑面<br />啊，这是怎样的诗境！</p> <p> Clouds Ravine</p> <p>Into Clouds Ravine<br />Sauntering over<br />Peaks and streams<br />Clouds and mist<br />Forest, springs, pines and rocks<br />As if into a three-dimensional space<br />All aesthetic feeling keep welling up</p> <p>Into Clouds Ravine<br />Watching the beautiful scenery of all hues and colours<br />Hearing the sounds of nature and voices<br />Smelling the fragrance of grasses and earth<br />Let a breeze and mist<br />Lightly touch my face<br />Oh, what a poetic land!</p> <p> 天都峰</p> <p>想登天都峰吗？<br />“于石壁外无路！”</p> <p>无路？无路<br />怎么登顶？</p> <p>走鲫鱼背吧！<br />那可是天险呀！<br />两侧悬崖千仞<br />风吹云涌<br />山摇地动……</p> <p>你敢？你敢<br />就成仙人了</p> <p>注：古人视此峰为“群仙所都”，取名“仙都峰”。</p> <p> Fairies-Resided Peck</p> <p>Do you want to reach Fairies-Resided Peck?<br />“No road but the cliff!”</p> <p>No road? Without a road<br />How to reach its top?</p> <p>Go through “carp’s Back”!<br />It is a natural barrier!<br />Cliffs high and steep on its both sides<br />When the wind rises and clouds rolls<br />The hills topple and the earth quakes…</p> <p>Do you dare to climb it? If you do<br />You will become an immortal!<br />Note: The ancients regarded this peck as “The residence of all fairies” and named it “Fairies Resided Peak”.</p> <p>@</p>
