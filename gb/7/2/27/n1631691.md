<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>就严正学案致美、加、澳、英、德、法等国首脑、议员及人权组织的信</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
	<p>【大纪元2月27日讯】发起者：<ahref="https://github.com/fdgxsg329/djy/blob/master/gb/tag/%E9%99%88%E7%94%A8%E6%9E%97.md#1">陈用林</a>、<ahref="https://github.com/fdgxsg329/djy/blob/master/gb/tag/%E7%9B%9B%E9%9B%AA.md#1">盛雪</a> (签名数: 122)</p>
<p>尊敬的xxx：</p>
<p>我们今天写信给您，是因为中国作家、画家<ahref="https://github.com/fdgxsg329/djy/blob/master/gb/tag/%E4%B8%A5%E6%AD%A3%E5%AD%A6.md#1">严正学</a>先生在中国被拘押，罪名是&#8221;颠覆国家政权罪&#8221;。</p>
<p>严先生于2006年10月18日被浙江省台州市公安局拘捕，并于11月1日以&#8221;颠覆国家政权罪&#8221;被正式起诉。台州市中级人民法院受理此案，目前严先生被关押在台州市路桥看守所。</p>
<p>看过法院文件的人表示，法院指控严先生涉嫌&#8221;秘密参加中国民主党&#8221;、在海外网站上刊登&#8221;煽动性&#8221;文章、呼吁为已故的作家和异议人士刘宾雁捐款并参与温岭农会筹备图谋&#8221;突破党禁&#8221;。<br />我们相信，这些指控本身就是对严先生权利的侵犯。我们认为，对他的拘押违反了中国1998年签署的《公民及政治权利国际公约》第19条。另外，中国本国《宪法》第35条规定：&#8221;中华人民共和国公民有言论、出版、集会、结社、游行、示威的自由。&#8221;</p>
<p>参加另一个政党怎可入罪！况且，严先生的律师李建强证实，严先生完全否认了他是中国民主党成员的指控。</p>
<p>严先生案原定于2月14日开庭，但因他的律师要求取证而延期。</p>
<p>我们对严先生的命运深表关注。如果罪名成立，根据中国的《刑法》第105条和第106条，他将会被处以至少10年的有期徒刑。他现已64岁。</p>
<p>我们对严先生现状有着最深切的懮虑，恳请您向中国政府提出此案并呼吁当局立即无条件释放他。我们担心，在中国这样的政治环境中，严先生将得不到公正的审判。以往类似案件的经验告诉我们，唯有国际压力，才会使严先生获得被释放的机会。</p>
<p>严先生已经有过一次自杀，以抗议当局对他的抓捕和囚禁。我们担懮，他会在绝望中再次自杀。</p>
<p>您的诚挚的<br />－－－－－－－－－－<br />H.E. XXX,</p>
<p>We are writing to you today regarding the Chinese writer and painter<br />Mr YAN Zhengxue, who is being held in China on charges of &#8220;subverting<br />state power&#8221;.</p>
<p>Mr YAN was arrested on 18 October 2006 by the Taizhou City Public<br />Security Bureau of Zhejiang Province and was officially charged on 1<br />November 2006 with &#8220;subverting state power&#8221;. The case is now in the<br />Taizhou City Intermediate People&#8217;s Court and Mr. YAN is held at the<br />Taizhou City Luqiao Detention Centre.</p>
<p>According to individuals who have sighted the court documents, this<br />charge is in connection with Mr YAN allegedly being &#8220;a secret member<br />of the China Democratic Party&#8221;, publishing &#8220;inciteful&#8221; articles on<br />overseas websites, calling for donations to a fund in honour of the<br />writer and dissident LIU Binyan (now deceased), and giving advice to<br />the Wenling Peasants Association on &#8220;breaking the ban on political<br />parties&#8221;.</p>
<p>We believe the charges themselves to be a violation of Mr YAN&#8217;s<br />rights. We regard him as being held in violation of Article 19 of the<br />International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights &#8211; to which China<br />became a signatory in 1998. In addition, Article 35 of China&#8217;s own<br />Constitution stipulates: &#8220;Citizens of the People&#8217;s Republic of China<br />enjoy freedom of speech, of the press, of assembly, of association, of<br />procession and of demonstration.&#8221;</p>
<p>While it should not be a crime to be a member of an alternate poitical<br />party, Mr YAN&#8217;s lawyer Mr LI Jianqiang attests that Mr YAN completely<br />denies the charge that he is a member of the China Democratic Party.</p>
<p>Mr YAN&#8217;s trial was scheduled to begin on Feburary 14, but this has<br />been deferred to allow his lawyer to collect evidence.</p>
<p>We are deeply concerned for the fate of Mr YAN. If found guilty,<br />according to Articles 105 and 106 of Chinese Penal Code, he will be<br />sentenced to a minimum of ten years in jail. He is now 64 years old.</p>
<p>We have the gravest fears for Mr YAN and ask you to raise his case<br />with the Chinese government and call for his immediate and<br />unconditional release. We are concerned that he will not be tried<br />fairly in a court free from political influence. We also know from<br />similar cases that it is only with international pressure that we have<br />any chance of winning Mr YAN&#8217;s release.</p>
<p>Mr YAN has already attempted suicide once in protest against his<br />arrest and incarceration. We fear he may do so again in desperation.</p>
<p>Faithfully yours,</p>
