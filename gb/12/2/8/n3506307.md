<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>看新闻学英语：中国地方政府负债“严重”</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p>Chinese Local Government Debt “Serious:” Debt Expert<br />债务专家：中国地方政府负债“严重”</p>
<p><a width="420" b="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/2sNwYM7ILhU" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></a><br />本则影音新闻出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻</p>
<p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1.	<B>trigger</B>: [ˋtr?g?] v.引起、触发<br />2.	<B>mortgage</B>: [ˋm?rg?d?] n.v.抵押<br />3.	<B>uncover</B>: [?nˋk?v?] v. 揭露、发现<br />4.	<B>irregularity</B>: [??r?gj?ˋl?r?t?] n.不法情事 <br />5.	<B>survey</B>: [s?ˋve] v.调查<br />6.	<B>reveal</B>: [r?ˋvil] v. 透露<br />7.	<B>sum up</B>: [b?d] [?p] ph.计算、总结<br />8.	<B>exaggerate</B>: [?gˋz?d???ret] v.夸大<br />9.	<B>statistics</B>: [st?ˋt?st?ks] n.统计<br />10.	<B>involve</B>: [?nˋvɑlv] v.牵涉、涉及<br />11.	<B>avert</B>: [?ˋv?t] v.避开、移开<br />12.	<B>toxic</B>: [ˋtɑks?k] adj.有毒的<br />13.	<B>estimate</B>: [ˋ?st??met] v.估计<br />14.	<B>bad loan</B>: [b?d lon] n.呆账</p>
<p>While the recent financial crisis in the West was<B> triggered</B> by the US sub-prime <B>mortgage</B> market, many analysts believe that in China it&#8217;s local government debt that could trigger a future financial crisis in the world’s second largest economy.<br />虽然最近西方金融危机，是由美国次级房贷市场引起，但许多分析家认为，中国地方政府的负债，可能引发未来世界第二大经济体的金融危机。</p>
<p>China’s audit office has uncovered over $80-billion of irregularities in local government debt, yet the problem may be even worse than what statistics show.<br />中国审计处已揭发地方政府超过 80亿美元的违法行为，然而问题可能比统计数据所显示的更糟。</p>
<p>[Yang Zhenghu, Director, Guangdong Social Sciences Association]:<br />“Of the several cities I have<B> surveyed</B>, the problem is far more serious than what has been<B> revealed</B>. The statistics bureau, when <B>summing up</B> this data, has a certain amount of freedom to<B> exaggerate</B>, they can adjust the <B>statistics </B>according to political preference. The longer this goes on, when we research, the data we find shows an even more difficult situation.”<br />广东省社会科学界联合会杨正浒说：<br />“在我所调查的几个城市，问题比已揭露的严重许多。统计局在计算数字时，有一定的自由空间可夸大，他们可依政治喜好，去调整统计数据。当我们继续研究更久，我们发现的数据，显示一种更艰难的情况。”</p>
<p>While the usual sources of income for local governments are decreasing, their spending is increasing. Yang Zhenghu says this debt based spending will eventually cause problems.<br />虽然地方政府一般收入来源减低，他们的开销却不断增加。杨正浒说，这种负债下的花费，最终会导致问题。</p>
<p>[Yang Zhenghu, Director, Guangdong Social Sciences Association]:<br />“The income of the local government from property sales has decreased a lot, but the overall income of the local government is still rising. With such a high debt level, in the end the burden will fall on the tax payers.”<br />广东省社会科学界联合会杨正浒说：<br />“地方政府从物业销售得来的收入减少许多，但整体收入仍在上涨。在如此高负债下，负担最终将落在纳税人身上。”</p>
<p>Yang says local governments are using a variety of methods to shift their debt around and maintain interest payments. Those methods mostly <B>involve</B>borrowing more money.<br />杨说，地方政府使用各种方法转移相关债务，维持利息支出。这些方法主要涉及借贷更多的钱。</p>
<p>[Yang Zhenghu, Director, Guangdong Social Sciences Association]:<br />“Different types of tools and methods can be used to make the data not look so bad. There are many methods to <B>avert</B> the risk [of default], originally [local governments] didn’t have mortgages, but now they mortgage some land or real estate, so their debts are less likely to be regarded as<B> toxic</B>.”<br />广东省社会科学界联合会杨正浒说：<br />“不同类型的工具和方法，可用来使数据看起来没那么糟。有许多方法可避免违约风险，原本地方政府没有抵押贷款，但现在他们抵押一些土地和房产，所以他们的债务，较不可能被视为有毒害的。”</p>
<p>Economists <B>estimate</B> that there are over $300-billion worth of <B>bad loans </B>within a total local government debt pile of over one point five trillion US dollars.<br />经济学家估计，在地方政府超过1.5兆美元的大量负债内，有超过 3,000亿美元价值的呆账。</p>
