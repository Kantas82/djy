<p><B>French &#8220;Spiderman&#8221; Climbs Hong Kong&#8217;s Hang Seng Bank</B><br />法国蜘蛛人攀爬香港恒生银行</p>

<p>French daredevil climber Alain Robert climbed the 27 stories of the 450 foot-high Hang Seng Bank headquarters in Hong Kong on Wednesday.<br />周三，法国蛮勇登山客Alain Robert，爬上450英尺高的香港恒生银行总部的27楼。</p>
<p>Robert, who earned the nickname &#8220;French Spiderman&#8221;<B> thanks to</B> his habit of <B>scaling</B> high buildings – usually without approval, did not<B> reveal</B> which building he planned to climb until he reached the site. This was to make sure his plan would not be stopped by authorities.<br />Robert由于攀爬高楼的习惯，赢得“法国蜘蛛人”的称号。通常他未经允许，在到达之前，不会透露计划攀爬的是哪栋建筑物。这是为确保他的计划，不会被有关当局阻止。</p>
<p>Building on the success of the climb, Robert plans to <B>conquer </B>the second-tallest building in Hong Kong, which is Two International Finance Centre.<br />建立在成功的攀爬，Robert计划征服香港第二高的建筑物，它是国际金融中心二期。</p>
<p>[Alain Robert, French Spiderman]:<br />&#8220;Hong Kong is a good place, you know, it’s the right spot, there are so many buildings and some of them are really fantastic piece, I am thinking especially about 2IFC.”<br />法国蜘蛛人Alain Robert说：<br />“你知道香港是个好地方，这是个好地点，有非常多的建筑物，其中有些真的是很了不起的作品，尤其我特别想到国际金融中心二期。”</p>
<p>It took Robert around 40 minutes to climb the Hang Seng Bank building, using just his <B>bare</B> hands and without any safety equipment.<br />Robert花了约 40分钟，用赤手攀爬恒生银行，没有任何安全设备。</p>
<p><B>A crowd of</B> pedestrians gathered at the foot of the building to watch the death-defying <B>stunt</B>.<br />许多路人群聚在大楼下，观看玩命特技。</p>
<p>[Kate Makusenko, Onlooker]:<br />&#8220;I was just passing by and I saw this. He is crazy.&#8221;<br />观众Kate Makusenko说：<br />“我只是路过看到。他疯了。”</p>
<p>Despite not having official permission to scale the walls of the building, Robert was allowed to walk away without <B>charges</B>, after a short discussion with the police and the management of the building.<br />尽管攀登大楼的外墙，没有得到官方同意，Robert与警察及大楼管方人员，在短暂讨论之后，被允许不起诉离开。</p>
<p>[Alain Robert, French Spiderman]:<br />&#8220;At the end of the day, I am very, very sure that there is absolutely zero damage and <B>in terms of</B> the law, what I did now is not considered as a crime, so that is the reason why I have been<B> released</B>.&#8221;<br />法国蜘蛛人Alain Robert说：<br />“在这天结束前，我非常、非常地肯定，绝对没有任何损害。在法律方面，我所做的不足以视为犯罪，这是为何我被释放的原因。”</p>
<p>Robert started his <B>vocation</B> young, climbing his first building at the age of 12 when he got locked out of his apartment. He then decided to mount the eight stories up to an open window.<br />Rober年轻就开始他的职业，12岁时，当时被锁在公寓外，就爬了他第一栋建筑物。他之后决定爬上八层楼高，到一个开着的窗口。</p>
<p>He has since climbed more than 80 buildings around the world, including Chicago&#8217;s Sears Tower and the Taipei 101 in Taiwan.<br />从那时到现在，他攀爬了80多栋世界各地的建筑物，包括芝加哥的西尔斯摩天大楼和台湾的台北101大楼。<br /> @*<br />***<br />本专栏由前卫英语李德良老师主编 www.davidlee.url.tw</p>
