<p>A 100-year-old British man ran his way to eight <B>sprinting</B> world records on Thursday and will try to set another when he <B>takes part in</B> Sunday&#8217;s Toronto Marathon.<br />周四，一名100岁英国男子跑出自己短跑的第8个世界纪录，并将参加周日的多伦多马拉松，再创另一项纪录。</p>
<p>Fauja Singh <B>demolished</B> the records for 100-year-old men in all eight sprinting distances.<br />Fauja Singh刷新100岁男子所有短跑距离的纪录。</p>
<p>The <B>Guinness</B> Book of World Records will be there on Sunday to <B>document</B> his <B>attempt </B>at completing a full marathon in the Toronto event. <br />金氐世界纪录周日将于那里，用文件证明他完成多伦多马拉松活动的企图心。</p>
<p>Singh will be the oldest of the 5000 <B>entrants </B>who will attempt the full 26-mile event.<br />Singh将是5,000名试图完成全程26英里活动参赛者中，最年长的一位。</p>
<p>He already owns a world record for fastest marathon run in the 90-year-old plus<B> category</B> after finishing in five hours, 40 minutes and one second in 2003 at the age of 92.<br />他已拥有一项世界纪录，就是90岁以上组，最快的马拉松跑者。2003年时他92岁，以5小时40分1秒的完成。</p>
<p>[Harmander Singh, Translator]: <br />&#8220;He just enjoys the run. The records are a bonus.&#8221; <br />翻译员Harmander Singh说：<br />“他只是在享受跑步。纪录是额外的奖赏。”</p>
<p>The <B>centenarian</B> was born on a farm in India in 1911 and started <B>competitive</B> running at the age of 89 after losing his wife and son.<br />百岁老人出生于 1911年的印度农场，失去妻子和儿子后，89岁开始赛跑。<br />@*</p>
