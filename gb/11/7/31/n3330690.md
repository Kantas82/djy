<p>Computer networking giant Cisco may be helping the Chinese regime <B>keep an eye on </B>its citizens &#8211; including monitoring political <B>dissidents</B>. <br />电脑网路巨头思科，可能会帮助中国政权监控公民─ ─包括监视持不同政见者。</p>
<p>Over the next two to three years, the city of Chongqing will install a complex network of surveillance equipment, including half a million security cameras. The project is<B> dubbed</B> &#8220;Peaceful Chongqing.&#8221; According to the Wall Street Journal, Cisco is likely to provide key pieces of equipment.<br />在未来两到三年内，重庆市将安装一个复杂的网路监控设备，包括50万台保全摄影机。该项目被称为“和平重庆”。据华尔街日报报导，思科可能提供其关键设备元件。 </p>
<p>Human rights groups are concerned the camera network will be used to further<B> stifle</B> human rights and political expression. <B>Amnesty</B> International researcher Corinna-Barbara Francis told the Journal that there is evidence Chinese authorities use video surveillance to, &#8220;<B>crack down </B>and then <B>criminalize</B> activity which should not be criminalized.&#8221;<br />人权组织担心，摄影机网路将被用来进一步压制人权，和人们的政治表达权利。大赦国际的研究员科琳娜 &#8211; 芭芭拉弗朗西斯告诉华尔街日报说，证据指出中国当局使用录影监控，来“打击那些本不应以犯罪论处的犯罪活动。”</p>
<p>Cisco general <B>counsel </B>Mark Chandler stated in a June blog post that they do not supply the CCP with equipment that has been &#8220;customized in any way&#8221; to <B>suppress </B>human rights.<br />思科总法律顾问马克钱德勒在6月的部落格文章中说，他们不提供中共“任何方式客制化”的设备助其压制人权。</p>
<p>After the 1989 Tiananmen Square <B>Massacre</B>, a U.S. law <B>barred</B> companies from exporting crime control products, like<B> fingerprinting</B> equipment, to China. But the restrictions don&#8217;t cover equipment with multiple uses, like security cameras.<br />经过 1989年的天安门大屠杀事件后，美国法律禁止公司行号出口犯罪控制产品到中国，如指纹识别设备等。但这项限制不包括具有多种用途的设备，如保全摄影机。<br />@*</p>
