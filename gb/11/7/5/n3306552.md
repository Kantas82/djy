<p><B>Wall Street Starts to Embrace Twitter</B><br />华尔街开始拥抱推特</p>
<p><embed src="http://english.ntdtv.com/p55.swf" width="600" b="450" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer" flashvars="config=http://english.ntdtv.com/p55config.xml&#038;file=http://media5.ntdtv.com/ml/english/news/wn/2011-06-20/20110620-WN-14_RREP-Wall-Street-Starts-to-Embrace-Twitter-V2.mp4&#038;abouttext=english.ntdtv.com&#038;aboutlink=http://english.ntdtv.com&#038;image=http://english.ntdtv.com/files/Content/20110620-WN-14-Wall-Street-Starts-to-Embrace-Twitter.jpg&#038;autostart=false"></embed><br />本则影音新闻出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻</p>
<p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1. <B>Embrace </B>:v. [?m&#8217;bres] 拥抱；采纳<br />2. <B>Barrier </B>: n. [&#8216;b?r??] 障碍<br />3. <B> Payoff</B>:n. [ˋpe??f]收益<br />4. <B>transaction </B>: n. [tr?nˋz?k??n] 交易 <br />4. <B>～fold </B>: [fold] …倍<br />5. <B>retail investor </B>: ph. 散户（投资者）<br />6. <B>go ahead </B>: ph. 许可<br />7. <B> roll out</B>:ph. 推行<br />8. <B> be wary of </B>: ph. 对…感到谨惕<br />9. <B>in large part</B>:ph. 在很大程度上<br />10. <B> compliance</B>:n. [k?m&#8217;pla??ns] 遵从<br />11. <B>comply with</B>:ph. 遵守<br />12. <B> leverage</B>:v. [&#8216;l?v??d?] 充份利用</p>
<p>Wall Street is starting to embrace Twitter.<br />华尔街现在开始拥抱推特了。</p>
<p>At the 140 character conference in New York City, financial executives talked about how <B>barriers</B>are coming down, and the<B> payoffs</B> are becoming more clear.<br />在纽约举办的《140 字元会议》上，财务长们讨论著如何克服阻碍，并且收益越来越明显。</p>
<p>Lori Feldman is the director of Branded and Social Media Marketing at Citi Global <B>Transaction Services.<br />洛瑞费尔德曼，是花旗全球交易服务的品牌暨社交媒体行销总监。</p>
<p>They started tweeting to institutional clients in late 2009.<br />2009年底，他们开始向法人（机构性）客户推特。</p>
<p>[Lori Feldman, Citi Global Transaction Services]: <br />&#8220;It was a great experiment, but it was a great experiment that paid off in that within the first week of our tweeting, that&#8217;s @citigts, we increased traffic to our website<B> five-fold</B>. Twenty percent of our tweets were re-tweeted.&#8221;<br />花旗全球交易服务的洛瑞费尔德曼说：“这是一项伟大的实验。这项伟大的实验在开始的第一周便已见效，就是 @citigts，我们增加了五倍网站流量。百分之二十的推特被转推 (re-tweeted)。”</p>
<p><B>Retail investors </B> will soon start to see more tweeting from Wall Street as well.<br />散户投资者也将很快可以看到更多来自华尔街的推特。</p>
<p>Morgan Stanley gave the <B>go ahead</B>for its army of more than 17,000 brokers to start using Twitter to communicate with clients &#8211; distributing research and content &#8211; and will <B>roll out </B> the program in the coming months.<br />摩根史丹利允许它超过 17,000名经纪人的庞大团队，开始使用推特与客户沟通—分送研究与内容─ ─并在未来几个月内，就要正式推行该计划。</p>
<p>Wall Street has<B> been wary of</B>social media &#8211; <B>in large part</B>because of the heavily regulated nature of the business.<br />华尔街向来对社交媒体很谨慎─ ─在很大程度上，是因为商业严格控管的本质。</p>
<p>Last year the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority- FINRA, established rules for social media use, but Wall Street has been slow to let brokers participate.<br />去年金融业监管局 (FINRA) 就建立了社交媒体使用规范，但华尔街让经纪人采用社群一直很缓慢。</p>
<p>Joanna Belbey is the co-founder of the BGK Group and advises companies on social media policy and <B>compliance</B>:<br />乔安娜贝尔贝是BGK集团的共同创办人，她对公司企业的社交媒体政策和守则有些建议。</p>
<p>[Joan Belbey, Co-founder of BGK Group]: <br />&#8220;This industry is highly regulated and there&#8217;s existing rules around supervision, suitability, advertising, communications bookkeeping where the firms need to take a really careful look at social media and each of the features and figure out how to <B>comply with </B> all the rules and regulations that apply to each one.&#8221;<br />BGK集团共同创办人乔安娜贝尔贝说：“这个行业是高度管制性的，而且在监管、合适性、广告、传播、簿记上有现行法规，让公司企业需要真正谨慎的看待社交媒体，以及它的每一项性质，弄清楚如何遵守所有的规则和适用于每一项性质的规范。”</p>
<p>The Twitter challenge: <B> leverage </B> the power of social media in an industry where they&#8217;ve got to play by rules, even when those rules just keep changing.<br />推特的挑战将是：如何在一个必需遵守规则的产业里，甚至当那些规则还在不断地改变之中，充分利用社会媒体的力量。<br />@*</p>
