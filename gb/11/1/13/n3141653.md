<p><B>New Year Celebrations in Asia</B><br />亚洲庆祝新年</p> <p><embed src="http://www.youmaker.com/v.swf" width="450" b="358" bgcolor="#FFFFFF" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer"allowFullScreen="true"flashvars="file=http://www.youmaker.com/video/v/nu/3b2737cc1f284180b48f16ac0d4ed3f7001.xml&#038;showdigits=true&#038;overstretch=fit&#038;autostart=false&#038;linkfromdisplay=false&#038;rotatetime=12&#038;repeat=list&#038;shuffle=false&#038;showfsbutton=false&#038;fsreturnpage=&#038;fullscreenpage=" /></p> <p>本则影音新闻出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻http://english.ntdtv.com/ntdtv_en/ns_asia/2011-01-01/331899723638.md#1</p> <p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1.	<B>fireworks</B>: [ˋfa?r?w?ks] n. 烟火大会<br />2.	<B>centerpiece</B>: [ˋs?nt??pis] n. 在正中央者<br />3.	<B>skyscraper</B>: [ˋska??skrep?] n. 摩天大楼<br />4.	<B>Reveler </B>: [ˋr?vl?] n. 狂欢者<br />5.	<B>balloon</B>: [b?ˋlun] n. 气球<br />6.	<B>calendar</B>: [ˋk?l?nd?] n. 历法</p> <p>Incredible <B>fireworks</B> displays seemed to be one of the best ways to ring in the New Year in many parts of Asia.<br />不可思议的烟火表演似乎是许多亚洲地区，在新年里最好的庆祝方式之一了。</p> <p>Thousands of people gathered in Hong Kong to watch one of the best displays which lit up the skyline against Victoria Harbour. A numbered countdown on the city&#8217;s tallest building signaled the start of the New Year.<br />数千人聚集在香港，看最好的烟火秀之一点亮维多利亚港的天空线。在香港最高大楼上数字的倒数，代表新年开始的讯号。</p> <p>It was a similar story in Taipei, Taiwan, where crowds gathered at a concert for the New Year celebrations. <br />在台湾台北有类似的故事，群众聚集在庆祝新年的音乐会上。</p> <p>The countdown was followed with a display of white colored fireworks from different locations around the city with the <B>centerpiece</B> display coming from the Taipei 101 <B>skyscraper</B>, the world&#8217;s second tallest building. <br />倒数声中伴随着来自全市不同地区白色的烟火，中间是全世界第二高的台北101摩天大楼。</p> <p>The celebrations also marked 100 years of Taiwan, officially known as the Republic of China.<br />这次也庆祝台湾100年，正式名称是中华民国。</p> <p>In Tokyo, thousands of people gathered at the historic Bhuddist Zojoji temple to celebrate the end of 2010 and the start of 2011.<br />在东京，数千人聚集在历史性的佛教增上寺，庆祝2010年的结束，与2011年的开始。</p> <p><B>Revelers </B>released <B>balloons</B> in celebration of the start of Year of the Rabbit, according to the Chinese <B>calendar</B>.<br />狂欢者释出无数的气球，庆祝中国历法上兔年的开始。</p> <p>In keeping with tradition the 15-ton temple bell was rung repeatedly to bring in the new year.<br />依于传统，15吨的钟持续不断地敲响，以带来新的一年。</p> <p>And in Beijing, China, residents gathered at the ancient Big Bell Temple to see in the New Year with the traditional ringing of one of the biggest bells in China.<br />在中国北京，居民们聚集在大钟古寺，在传统上敲响中国最大钟之一（译按：永乐大钟）的钟声中，迎接新的一年。<br />@*</p> <p>本专栏由前卫英语David Lee主编 www.davidlee.url.tw</p>
