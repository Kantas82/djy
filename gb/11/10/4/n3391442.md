<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p><B>Rare Beatles Photos Up for Auction</B><br />披头四罕见照片拍卖</p>
<p><embed src="http://english.ntdtv.com/p55.swf" width="600" b="450" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer" flashvars="config=http://english.ntdtv.com/p55config.xml&#038;file=http://media5.ntdtv.com/ml/english/news/wn/2011-09-15/20110915-WN-14_RREP-Rare-Beatles-Photos-Up-for-Auction.mp4&#038;abouttext=english.ntdtv.com&#038;aboutlink=http://english.ntdtv.com&#038;image=http://english.ntdtv.com/files/Content/20110915-WN-14-Rare-Beatles-Photos-Up-for-Auction.jpg&#038;autostart=false"></embed><br />本则影音新闻出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻</p>
<p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1.	<B>Rare [r?r] </B>: adj. 罕见的<br />2.	<B>Auction</B>: [ˋ?k??n] n. 拍卖<br />3.	<B>fab </B>: [f?b] adj. 极好的；传说的 (=fabulous)<br />4.	<B>polished</B>: [ˋpɑl??t] adj. 精炼的<br />5.	<B>slick</B>: [sl?k] adj. 整齐的<br />6.	<B>outfit </B>: [ˋa?t?f?t] n. 成套服装<br />7.	<B>entity</B>: [ˋ?nt?t?] n. 实体<br />8.	<B>rugged</B>: [ˋr?g?d] adj. 粗犷的<br />9.	<B>bidder</B>: [ˋb?d?] n. 投标者<br />10.	<B>negative</B>: [ˋn?g?t?v] n. 底片<br />11.	<B>unprecedented</B>: [?nˋpr?s??d?nt?d] adj. 空前的<br />12.	<B>remarkable</B>: [r?ˋmɑrk?b!] adj. 卓越的<br />13.	<B>gem</B>: [d??m] n. 瑰宝</p>
<p>Young and unknown &#8211; the Beatles in Hamburg, Germany in 1960.<br />1960年在德国汉堡的披头四乐团─ ─年轻且还未成名。</p>
<p>Images created by German photographer Astrid Kirchherr who befriended the <B>fab</B> four and was engaged to Beatles bass player Stuart Sutcliffe until his death in 1962.<br />影像出自德国摄影师阿斯特丽德柯区儿。当时她结识传说中这四人，并且和披头四贝斯手史都华萨特克利夫结为连理，直到他1962年去世。</p>
<p>Now, her photographs will be auctioned in New York City.<br />现在，她的摄影作品将在纽约市进行拍卖。 </p>
<p>Arlan Ettinger, President of auction house Guernsey&#8217;s, says the pictures show the Beatles before a more<B> polished </B>foursome became famous.<br />格恩西拍卖行的总裁阿尔兰艾丁格说，照片显现了披头四在成为更精炼的著名四人乐团之前的样子。</p>
<p>[Arlan Ettinger, President of Guernsey&#8217;s]:<br />&#8220;When the Beatles first arrived on the scene, and <B>was sort of around</B>, they were very sort of a<B> slick</B>-looking group. They all wore the same <B>outfits</B>, their hair was cut the same, they became a single <B>entity</B>. What you see here, you see visions of them in sort of their rawest, <B>rugged </B>best.&#8221;<br />格恩西拍卖行的总裁阿尔兰艾丁格说：“当披头四首先来到现场，有点儿像是四处逛逛，他们的外表都看起来很整齐。他们都穿同样的成套服装，发型也剪一样，他们成为了单一实体。你在这里看到的，你可以看到他们呈现出的某种最原始、最粗犷的视野。”</p>
<p>In addition to the photographs, the winning bidder will also acquire the <B>negatives</B> and copyright, giving the buyer <B>unprecedented </B>access to the Beatles says Ettinger.<br />艾丁格说，除了相片外，得标人也将获得底片和版权，给买方前所未有的披头四存取权。</p>
<p>[Arlan Ettinger, President of Guernsey&#8217;s]:<br />&#8220;It allows you to control one of these <B>remarkable</B> images of John Lennon, Paul McCartney, or the other Beatles and that&#8217;s what&#8217;s so unique about this offering. It&#8217;s not just the photograph itself.&#8221;<br />格恩西拍卖行的总裁阿尔兰艾丁格说：“它让您可以应用约翰列侬、保罗麦卡尼，或其他披头四成员等其中这些卓越影像之一，这就是这项拍卖独特之处。它不是只有照片本身而已。”</p>
<p>And even though fans can&#8217;t buy love, they can bid on these Beatles <B>gems</B>.<br />尽管歌迷买不到他们的爱，但他们可以竞标这些披头四瑰宝。<br />@*</p>
