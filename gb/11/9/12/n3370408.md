<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p>Ailing Indonesians Lay on Train Tracks for Free Treatment<br />生病的印尼人卧躺火车轨道 为得免费治疗<br /><embed src="http://english.ntdtv.com/p55.swf" width="600" b="450" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer" flashvars="config=http://english.ntdtv.com/p55config.xml&#038;file=http://media5.ntdtv.com/ml/english/news/wn/2011-08-17/20110817-WN-13_Ailing-Indonesians-Lay-on-Train-Tracks-for-Free-Treatment.mp4&#038;abouttext=english.ntdtv.com&#038;aboutlink=http://english.ntdtv.com?=http://english.ntdtv.com/files/Content/20110817-WN-13-Ailing-Indonesians-Lay-on-Train-Tracks-for-Free-Treatment574470.jpg&#038;autostart=false"></embed><br />本则影音新闻出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻</p>

<p>The practice of lying across train tracks is not normally<B> associated</B>with good health, but for an increasing number of Indonesians, it&#8217;s become an effective <B>alternative</B>to <B>conventional</B>medical <B>therapy</B>for easing aches and pains.<br />卧火车轨这种做法，一般不会与健康连想在一起，印尼人却为数增加，它已成为一种有效替代传统医疗，舒缓疼痛的另类治疗。</p>
<p>It&#8217;s all about the<B> electricity</B>. <br />这一切与电流有关。</p>
<p> They say the mild current that passes through their bodies when they lie across the track, makes pain go away and eases the<B> symptoms </B> of a range of conditions from<B> rheumatism </B> to<B> diabetes</B>.<br />他们说，当横卧轨道，温和的电流穿过身体时，会让疼痛消失及减轻，从风湿到糖尿病的很多病症都可以。</p>
<p>One particular track in western Jakarta has become popular with people who are either either too poor to afford conventional medical treatments or for whom those treatments have been ineffective.<br />尤其在雅加达西部一个轨道，变得很受人欢迎，那些是太穷付不起传统医疗费用，或（医院）治疗已无效的人。</p>
<p>[Husin, Local User]: <br />&#8220;I had an accident and caused a fracture in my bone, I could not do any activities before. But now I can wake up by myself and walk. Thanks to the therapy I did for 2.5 months recently.&#8221;<br />当地使用者Husin说：<br />“我因车祸造成骨折，之前不能做任何活动。但现在可以自己起床和走路。感谢自己近二个半月所做的治疗。”</p>
<p>[Inah, Satisfied User]: <br />&#8220;I had<B> uric</B>acid in my hand joint which was hard to move, but thank god now it&#8217;s getting better.&#8221;<br />满意的使用者Inah说：<br />“我因手关节尿酸高，所以很难移动，但感谢上天，现在越来越好。”</p>
<p>According to government statistics more than 30 million Indonesians are officially poor. <br />根据政府官方统计，超过3千万印尼人为穷人。</p>
<p>The country&#8217;s national health service is often accused of being too expensive for its poor. <br />该国的国家健康服务，常被指控对于贫穷人太过昂贵。</p>
<p>As a result alternative therapies are <B>abound</B>throughout the country. <br />因此另类疗法充斥全国各地。</p>
<p>No studies have been conducted on the medical benefits of electricity from the railway tracks.<br />没有研究显示来自火车轨道的电流会带来疗效。</p>
<p>But a local medical practitioner is concerned.<br />但当地医生关注此事。</p>
<p>[Dr. Dwi Yani, Chief, Local Health Center]:<br />&#8220;Electricity is used for special cases and severe cases but it&#8217;s not used for all diseases. But here they use the therapy for all kinds of diseases and this situation will impact on physical body, brain and heart.&#8221;<br />当地健康中心主任，Dwi Yani医生说：<br />“电流是用于特殊或重症病例，并不适用于所有疾病。但在这里，他们在各种疾病上都使用这种治疗，这情况会对身体、大脑和心脏造成影响。”</p>
<p>But for officials trying to keep the trains running safely and on time, the &#8220;therapy&#8221; seekers are an<B> impediment</B>.<br />但对于试图维持列车运行安全和准时的官员来说，寻找“治疗”的人是一种阻碍。</p>
<p>[Suardi, Chain Station Chief]: <br />&#8220;We already see that the activities are very dangerous for them and they are also disturbing train travel.&#8221; <br />火车站站长Suardi说：<br />“我们已看到，这活动对他们很危险，而且他们己妨碍火车行驶。”</p>
<p>Officials say there have been no accidents involving trains and therapy-seekers so far.<br />官员表示，目前尚未出现与火车和寻求治疗相关的意外发生。<br />@*</p>
