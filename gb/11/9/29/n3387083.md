<p>London Zoo Animals Weigh in and Measure up<br />伦敦动物园帮动物量测身高体重</p>
<p><embed src="http://english.ntdtv.com/p55.swf" width="600" b="450" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer" flashvars="config=http://english.ntdtv.com/p55config.xml&#038;file=http://media5.ntdtv.com/ml/english/news/wn/2011-08-26/20110826-WN-16_London-Zoo-Animals-Weigh-in-and-Measure-up.mp4&#038;abouttext=english.ntdtv.com&#038;aboutlink=http://english.ntdtv.com&#038;image=http://english.ntdtv.com/files/Content/20110826-WN-16-London-Zoo-Animals-Weigh-in-and-%20Measure-up.jpg&#038;autostart=false"></embed><br />本则影音新闻出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻</p>
<p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1.	<B>Cut out</B>: ph. 安排<br />2.	<B>Monumental</B>: adj. [?mɑnj?ˋm?nt!] 巨大而重要的<br />3.	<B>Golden lion headed tamarin</B>: ph. 金毛狮狨<br />4.	<B>Tamarin </B>:n. [ˋt?m?r?n] 狨猴<br />5.	<B>Scale</B>: n. [skel] 秤<br />6.	<B>Tamandua</B>: n. [t??m?nd?ˋwɑ] 小食蚁兽<br />7.	<B>Lumber </B>:v. [ˋl?mb?] 笨重地移动<br />8.	<B>Fuss </B>:n. [f?s] 忙乱；大惊小怪<br />9.	<B>Treat </B>:n. [trit] 请客</p>
<p>How tall or short, fat or thin? London Zoo keepers had their work <B>cut out </B>on Thursday conducting the annual weight and height measurements of all their animals.<br />多高？多矮？多胖？多瘦？伦敦动物园的管理员们周四安排了一项工作，要帮所有的动物进行年度测量身高和体重。</p>
<p>It&#8217;s a <B>monumental</B> task to weigh and measure more than 750 different species, including lions, tigers, penguins and snakes.<br />要帮750种不同的物种测量及秤重是一件巨大而重要的工作，这些物种包括狮子、老虎、企鹅和蛇。</p>
<p>First up were the cheeky<B> golden lion headed tamarins</B>, who needed no encouragement to jump all over the specially adapted <B>scales</B>.<br />首先亮相的是厚脸皮的金毛狮狨，不需别人鼓舞就直接跳上特殊改装的磅秤上。</p>
<p>Cuddly Tammy the<B> tamandua</B>, or tree ant eater, <B> lumbered</B> along to see what all the <B>fuss </B>was about and happily spent a long time on the scales in front of photographers. <br />可爱的小食蚁兽塔米，或叫树蚁食者，则是笨重地沿路爬行，想看看是什么好大惊小怪的，然后开心地在磅秤上和摄影师面前花上一段长时间。</p>
<p>She weighed in at around 19 pounds.<br />她秤约有19磅重。</p>
<p>[Nicky Jargo, Senior Keeper, London Zoo]: <br />&#8220;She&#8217;s probably a little bit heavy this morning following all that banana that she had as a<B> treat </B>on the scales, but she&#8217;s a good healthy weight at the moment.&#8221;<br />伦敦动物园资深管理员妮基贾歌说：“她今天早上可能有一点点重，因为她在磅秤上吃了不少我们请她的香蕉，不过她现在的体重很健康。”</p>
<p><B>【新闻关键字】</B></p>
<p>1.	<B>Tortoise </B>:n. [ˋt?rt?s] 陆龟；乌龟<br />2.	<B>Tarantula </B>: n. [t?ˋr?nt??l?] 狼蛛；鸟蛛<br />3.	<B>Stick insect </B>: ph. 竹节虫<br />4.	<B>Milk snake </B>: ph. 奶蛇<br />5.	<B>Meticulous</B>:adj. [m?ˋt?kj?l?s] 严密的；细致的<br />6.	<B>Poorly</B>:adj. [ˋp?rl?] 身体不佳的<br />7.	<B>Indication </B>: n. [??nd?ˋke??n] 征兆；指示<br />8.	<B>Pregnancy </B>: n. [ˋpr?gn?ns?] 怀孕<br />9.	<B>statistics</B>:ph. 统计资料</p>
<p>The heavyweight of the<B> tortoise </B>world, the giant Galapagos tortoise, &#8220;Dirk&#8221; weighed in as a respectable 371 pounds.<br />在重量级的乌龟世界，巨型加拉巴戈象龟“德克”，量出了一个可敬的371磅。</p>
<p>A carrot was all that was needed to get Dirk onto the scales, but some creatures are far more difficult to deal with. <br />胡萝卜是所有能够让德克爬上磅秤的原因，但有些动物则更加难以处理。</p>
<p>[Nicky Jargo, Senior Keeper, London Zoo]:<br />&#8220;Some of the harder ones are some of the flighty animals, so for example some of the smaller birds who don&#8217;t like to sit still for too long.&#8221;<br />伦敦动物园资深管理员妮基贾歌说：“比较难的是一些会飞的动物，例如一些比较小的鸟类，它们不喜欢坐着不动太长时间。”</p>
<p>The insects are measured rather than weighed. <br />昆虫则是测量身长而非秤重。</p>
<p>Jacqueline the <B>tarantula</B> was 4 inches. <br />狼蛛贾桂琳有4吋长。</p>
<p>The <B>stick insect </B>came in at around 12 inches &#8211; the length of the keeper&#8217;s ruler.<br />竹节虫则是大约12吋 – 和管理员尺的长度一样。</p>
<p>Once keepers managed to straighten out Stripey the<B> milk snake</B>, they discovered he was 39 inches long.<br />当管理员们设法把奶蛇史翠皮撑长时，他们发现他有39吋长。</p>
<p>The<B> meticulous </B>recording of heights and weights helps the zoo monitor the animals&#8217; health and general well-being.<br />这些严谨的长度和重量记录，能帮助动物园监视动物的健康和一般福利。</p>
<p>[Nicky Jargo, Senior Keeper, London Zoo]: <br />&#8220;Obviously the animals can&#8217;t tell us if they are <B>poorly</B>, so we do have to keep a very good visual eye on them and weight change, sudden weight change, is a really good <B>indication </B>of any health problems there might be. And also monitoring any possible<B> pregnancies</B> as well.&#8221;<br />伦敦动物园资深管理员妮基贾歌说：“很明显地，动物身体状况不好时，他们不能告诉我们，所以我们确实需要用眼睛好好地注视他们。体重的变化、体重的突变，是任何可能的健康问题，一个非常好的征兆。此外我们也监控任何可能的怀孕。”</p>
<p>The information is shared with other zoos to help better understand each species. <br />这些资讯也会和其它动物园共享，以帮助更加了解各类物种。</p>
<p>The logs of vital <B>statistics</B> help with field conservation projects where wild animals are monitored to better understand behavior, health and even an animal&#8217;s age.<br />这些重要的统计资料纪录，能帮助实地保育计划中进行的野生动物监测，以便更好地理解动物的行为、健康，甚至动物年龄。<br />@*</p>
