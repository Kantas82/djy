<h6></h6>

<p>Thousands of Hong Kong activists <B>rallied</B> with a &#8220;Goddess of Democracy&#8221; replica statue on Sunday. It&#8217;s to <B>commemorate</B> the 1989 Tiananmen Square Massacre—called the &#8220;June 4th Incident&#8221; in Chinese—where hundreds of pro-democracy students were killed.<br />周日数千名香港活动人士聚集在复制的“民主女神”雕像旁。这是为纪念1989年天安门大屠杀，中文称为“六四天安门事件”，数百名支持民主的学生在那里被杀害。</p>
<p>Hong Kong Legislator Lee Cheuk-Yuen speaks about what the statue stands for. <br />香港立法委员李焯源谈到雕像所代表的象征意义。</p>
<p>[Lee Cheuk-Yuen, Hong Kong Legislator]:<br />&#8220;This is a <B>symbol </B>of defiance on the part of</B> the Hong Kong people to demand the<B> vindication</B> of June 4 and democracy in China. It also has another meaning being that this year is the darkest age for Chinese human rights, and there are waves and waves of arrests and <B>suppression</B> of Chinese human rights defenders.&#8221;<br />香港立法会议员李焯源说：<br />“香港人民要求六四及民主在中国无罪，是忤逆当权者的象征。它也有另一种意义─今年是中国人权最黑暗的时代，一波又一波的逮捕及镇压中国人权的捍卫者。”</p>
<p>This year, the <B>persecution</B> of dissident artist Ai Weiwei and Nobel Peace Prize winner Liu Xiaobo furthers concerns that the Chinese regime is going backwards on human rights.<br />今年，异议份子艺术家艾未未和诺贝尔和平奖得主刘晓波的受迫害，更让人进一步忧心中国政权在人权上的倒退。</p>
<p>Students, politicians, and unionists joined the rally to<B> mourn </B>the lives lost in 1989, and speak out against the Chinese regime. <br />学生、政治人物和工会人士参加集会，悼念1 989年逝去的生命，大声疾呼反对中共政权。</p>
<p>One protestor says he&#8217;s fortunate to be able to protest here—because his friends in mainland China cannot.<br />一名抗议者说，他很幸运能在这里抗议，因为他在大陆的朋友无法这么做。</p>
<p>[James Hon, Protester]:<br />&#8220;Hong Kong up till now is the only Chinese territory that people can come out to the street and voice our protest and we are lucky to have this freedom.&#8221;<br />抗议者James Hon说：<br />“直到现在，香港是中国领土内，唯一能够让人民上街发声抗议的地方，我们很幸运有这个自由。”</p>
<p>But, even in Hong Kong, protests have not always gone smoothly. Last year, 13 protestors were arrested for<B> putting up</B> two similar statues, which were then <B>confiscated </B>by police.<br />但是，即使在香港，抗议活动并不是一直很顺利。去年，有13位抗议者因为建造两座类似的雕像被逮捕，之后雕像就被警察没收。<br />@*<br />本专栏由前卫英语李德良老师主编 www.davidlee.url.tw</p>
