<p>China’s massive high-speed rail network is being called unsafe, unprofitable and <B>corrupt</B>.<br />中国庞大的高速铁路网被称为不安全的、不赚钱的、和贪污腐败的。</p>
<p>The Chinese Railways Ministry Chief, Liu Zhijun<B> was in charge of</B> the construction of the railway, spanning almost 8,100 miles across China and reportedly costing $395 billion U.S. dollars.<br />中国铁路部长刘志军负责铁路建造，几乎跨越中国8,100英里，据报导耗资3950亿美元。</p>
<p>Liu was fired In February. He’s now <B>behind bars</B> and under investigation for “severe violations of discipline.” During his seven years as chief, he <B>embezzled</B> a personal fortune of article content begin -->20 billion U.S. dollars.<br />刘志军在二月被开除。他现在身陷囹圄，因“严重违反纪律”而被调查中。他当部长在位7年期间，个人贪污1200亿美元财产。</p>
<p>Corruption is not the only problem <B>plaguing</B> China’s railways. Experts say <B>shoddy </B>materials were used to cut costs. According to a source for the New York Times, the concrete bases for the tracks were so <B>cheaply </B>made, with insufficient hardening <B>agents</B> that the tracks would<B> warp</B> in five years.<br />贪污并不是困扰中国铁路唯一的问题。专家说，还有使用劣质材料以降低成本。据纽约时报消息指出，混凝土基地的轨道是廉价制造，用不够硬度的作用剂，轨道可能在五年内弯曲。</p>
<p>On top of the safety risks, high-speed trains are more expensive. A passenger on the Shanghai-Hangzhou line last year said first class was completely empty and he was the only passenger in his second-class cabin.<br />安全风险最高，高速火车更昂贵。去年一位乘坐上海杭州线的旅客说，头等舱完全是空的，他是唯一在二等舱的乘客。</p>
<p>With high construction costs, safety risks and dissatisfied passengers, the <B>debt-ratio</B> of the Chinese regime’s Railway ministry, which <B>stands at</B> about 70 percent, could face an even more <B>dismal</B> future.<br />由于建设成本高、安全风险和不满的乘客，中共政权的铁道部负债比率约是70%，可能会面临更加惨淡的未来。<br />@*</p>
