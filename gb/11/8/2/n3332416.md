<p>Japan: Prices Up, But Deflation Not Over<br />日本：物价指数上涨，但通货紧缩未结束</p>
<p><embed src="http://english.ntdtv.com/p55.swf" width="600" b="450" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer" flashvars="config=http://english.ntdtv.com/p55config.xml&#038;file=http://media5.ntdtv.com/ml/english/news/ab/2011-05-27/20110527-AB-14_RREP-Japan-Prices-Up-But-Deflation-Not-Over-V3.mp4&#038;abouttext=english.ntdtv.com&#038;aboutlink=http://english.ntdtv.com&#038;image=http://english.ntdtv.com/files/Content/20110527-AB-14_RREP-Japan-Prices-Up-But-Deflation-Not-Over821285.jpg&#038;autostart=false"></embed><br />本则影音新闻出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻</p>
<p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1.	<B>deflation</B>: n. [d?ˋfle??n] 通货紧缩<br />2.	<B>price gauge</B>: n. [pra?s ged?] 物价指数<br />3.	<B>be far from</B>: ph. [bi] [fɑr frɑm] 一点也没、绝没有<br />4.	<B>underlying price</B>: n. [??nd?ˋla??? pra?s] 基础价格<br />5.	<B>edge</B>: v. [?d?] 徐徐移动<br />6.	<B>upward</B>: adj. [ˋ?pw?d] 向上的<br />7.	<B>in line with</B>: ph. [skwiz] 挤压、紧缩<br />12.	<B>consumer sentiment</B>: n. [k?nˋsjum s?nt?m?nt] 消费者信心</p>
<p>Japan&#8217;s main <B>price gauge</B> rose for the first time in more than two years, but the country&#8217;s battle with deflation <B>is far from </B>over.<br />日本主要物价指数，两年多来首次上扬，但与通货紧缩之战，离结束还远。</p>
<p>Core consumer prices rose 0.6 percent in the year to April, marking the first rise since December 2008.<br />今年到四月为止，核心消费者物价上涨0.6％，是2008年12月以来首次上涨。</p>
<p>Factors such as rising commodity costs were behind the increase in the Consumer Price Index, but data shows<B> underlying prices</B> and consumption in Japan still remain weak following the March earthquake.<br />消费物价指数上升的背后因素，是商品成本上升，但数据显示日本基础价格和消费，继三月发生地震后，仍然疲弱。</p>
<p>Core consumer prices are likely to continue to <B>edge upward</B> due to high oil prices, <B> in line with</B> Bank of Japan forecasts<B> issued</B> last month.<br />核心消费物价由于高油价，很可能继续往向上攀升，这与日本央行上月发布的预测一致。</p>
<p>But economists say the rise in the<B> headline</B> price gauge does not mean Japan will <B>escape from nagging </B>deflation, given a weak labor market and low corporate hiring.<br />但经济学家说，从疲软的劳动市场和低迷的企业雇用可得知，主要物价指数的上升，并不表示日本将摆脱通货紧缩的困扰。</p>
<p>Company profits have also been <B>squeezed </B>by poor<B> consumer sentiment</B>, with many Japanese households still unwilling to spend and searching for cheaper goods.<br />公司的获利也因消费者情绪低迷而削减，许多日本家庭仍不愿消费，并寻找更便宜的物品。<br />@*</p>
