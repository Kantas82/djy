It&#8217;s a rollercoaster ride without the fun involved in it.&#8221; <br />“不好玩，那是一定的，不是很好玩。涉入其中，像是云霄飞车，但一点也不好玩。”</p> <p>[Frank Pimpinella, Works on Wall Street]:<br />&#8220;It&#8217;s ridiculous. It&#8217;s one day it&#8217;s up for 500, then it&#8217;s down. It&#8217;s up. I&#8217;m not sure what the markets going to do, but it&#8217;s all in Europe. Europe&#8217;s our problem, I believe. So, anybody&#8217;s guess how it&#8217;s going to go today.&#8221;<br />“很可笑，一天内上扬500点，然后又下跌500点。我不确定市场将要怎么走，但全看欧洲，我相信欧洲是我们的问题所在。所以任何人都在猜，市场今天会怎么走。”</p> <p>Europe is indeed a worry. Markets have been beaten down most recently on concerns about the French banking system&#8217;s exposure to troubled<B> euro zone sovereign</B> debt. But talk of an <B>upcoming </B>meeting between France and Germany, Europe&#8217;s two biggest economies, are giving European markets a lift, and is another factor in Wall Street&#8217;s early surge.<br />欧洲的确是个担忧，市场最近被打下挫，是因忧虑法国银行系统的曝光，对已有麻烦的欧元区主权债务。但是谈到一场欧洲两大经济体法国与德国之间即间来到的会议，还给欧洲市场一剂振奋，这也是华尔街早盘上扬的另一项因素。<br />@*</p> <p>本专栏由前卫英语李德良老师主编 www.davidlee.url.tw</p> <p></p>
