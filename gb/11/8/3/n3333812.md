<p><B>British Newspapers Run Murdoch Apology</B><br />英国报纸刊登梅铎道歉</p>

<p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1.	<B>run</B>: v. [r?n]刊登<br />2.	<B>wrongdoing</B>: n. [ˋr??ˋdu??]罪行、犯罪<br />3.	<B>suffer</B>: v. [ˋs?f?]遭受、承受<br />4.	<B>hack</B>: v. [h?k]劈、砍<br />5.	<B>hold</B>: v. <p>News Corp Chief Executive Rupert Murdoch used advertisements in national newspapers on Saturday to apologise for the &#8216;News of the World&#8217;s &#8220;serious <B>wrongdoing</B>.&#8221;<br />新闻集团首席执行长鲁伯特梅铎，周六在全国性报纸上刊登广告，为世界新闻报的“严重罪行”道歉。</p>
<p>The media tycoon said he was &#8220;deeply sorry&#8221; for the <B>suffering</B> caused to victims of the news <B>hacking</B> scandal.<br />传媒大亨说，对遭受窃听消息丑闻之苦的受害者，我“深感歉意”。</p>
<p>The advert was running in Saturday&#8217;s Daily Mail, Daily Telegraph, Financial Times, Guardian, Independent, Sun and Times, News International said.<br />新闻国际说，该广告在周六的每日邮报、每日电讯报、金融时报、卫报、独立报，太阳报和泰晤士报上刊出。</p>
<p>&#8220;The News of the World was in the business of <B>holding </B>others to <B>account</B>. It failed when it came to itself,&#8221; Murdoch wrote in the article, which was signed &#8220;Sincerely, Rupert Murdoch.&#8221;<br />他说：“《世界新闻报》一直以来的工作是要人们为自己的行为负责，自己却没做到。”梅铎在文章中写道，署名“鲁伯特．梅铎敬上”。</p>
<p>&#8220;We are sorry for the serious wrongdoing that occurred. We are deeply sorry for the hurt suffered by the individuals <B>affected</B>,&#8221; he added.<br />“我们很抱歉发生这种严重错误。对受波及而受伤害的人，我们感到非常难过。”他接着说。 </p>
<p>&#8220;In the coming days, as we take further concrete steps to resolve these issues and make amends for the damage they have caused, you will hear more from us.&#8221;<br />“未来日子，我们会采取具体措施解决这些问题，并对所造成的损害作修正，各位将会听到我们更多的消息。”</p>
<p>On Friday, in an <B>attempt </B>to gain control of a scandal rocking his company and <B>inching </B>its way ever-closer to his son and newspaper empire heir James Murdoch, the media <B>baron</B> apologized in person to victims of criminal phone hacking by one of his <B>tabloids</B> and accepted the resignations of News Corp&#8217;s top two newspaper executives, Rebekah Brooks and Les Hinton.<br />周五，为了将动摇他公司并且一步步影响他儿子的丑闻得到（损害）控制，媒体大亨为了他旗下小报违法窃听电话，亲自向受害者道歉，并接受新闻集团两位高层新闻主管，利百加?布鲁克斯和莱斯?韩丁的辞呈。</p>
<p>The crisis has broken the <B>grip</B> that 80 year old Murdoch had over British politics for three decades, as leaders from Margaret Thatcher, through Labour&#8217;s Tony Blair to current Conservative Prime Minister David Cameron sought his support.<br />这场危机使得80的岁梅铎在英国政坛呼风唤雨30年的时代告终，政坛领袖柴契尔夫人，工党布莱尔，到现在的保守党首相大卫?卡梅伦，都曾寻求他的支持。<br />@*</p>
