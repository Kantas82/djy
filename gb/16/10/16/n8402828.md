<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>许其正：那座塔</h2>
<img width="600" src="https://i.epochtimes.com/assets/uploads/2011/04/100808123723100445-600x400.jpg" />
<h6>远处江河之滨，有光芒闪烁，像残星将坠一般。(摄影：Ben Heys/Fotolia)
</h6>
<hr>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>在旧居屋后不远处那片牧场里<br />
在记忆深处那片童年广场里<br />
那座<ahref="https://github.com/ghmtyd332/djy/blob/master/gb/tag/%E5%A1%94.md#1">塔</a>坚强挺立着<br />
昂头，硬颈，焕发不屈……以至而今</p>
<p>曾经，许多牛只在它近旁吃草，翻水，斗角，玩青春的游戏<br />
曾经，许多不同年龄、不同性别的牧人在它近旁烙下脚印，飘飞欢声、笑语、歌唱<br />
曾经，在它近旁，随着春夏秋冬的轮转而草荣草枯，虫生虫灭<br />
曾经，群鸟在它的头顶筑巢，栖居，鸣唱，嬉戏，结婚，生子<br />
曾经，不少热恋中人把爱的山盟海誓镌刻它的水泥支柱上<br />
曾经，它经年累月和我的思绪纠缠又纠缠，甚至让我携带着游走四方<br />
曾经呀曾经……</p>
<p>岁月的铁锤不住猛敲猛打<br />
风雨的剑戢不住猛砍猛刺<br />
那片牧场已经沧海桑田变为蔗园<br />
那片童年牧场已经不再童年<br />
许多事物都被历史的洪流淹没，推倒，改写<br />
它还能挺立多久呢？</p>
<p>尽管昂头硬颈吧！<br />
唉，逃不过的<br />
我隐约看见它驼着背的龙钟老态、点点老人斑、深深皱纹和灰白鬓发<br />
甚至隐约听见它的声声咳嗽，忧郁叹息<br />
想必是黄昏和寒冬正冉冉披挂在它的身上吧！<br />
只能以焕发不屈来代替青春勃发，意气昂扬了</p>
<p><strong>That pagoda</strong> ? ? Hsu ChiCheng</p>
<p>On that piece of the pasture no far near my old abode<br />
On my childhood square deep in my memory<br />
That pagoda stands straighten up<br />
Lifts up the head, stubbornness, shines not to surrender to…till now</p>
<p>Once, there were cattle take grass, bathing, fight with horn, play youth game near it<br />
Once, there were many herdsmen with different age and sex prints footprints, fly song, joyful words, and sing song near it<br />
Once, there were grasses flourish and wither, insect born and become extinct follow the turning of the season near it<br />
Once, there were birds nest, perch, sing, play, merry and give birth to baby bird near it<br />
Once, there were bustle lovers sculpt on the cement post their pledge of faithfulness and love<br />
Once, it was tangle and tangle with my mind whole the years, even let me carry it to tour everywhere of the world<br />
Oh once and once…</p>
<p>The iron hammer of the year knocks and beats ceaselessly fiercely<br />
The wind storm of the sword and halberd chop and sting ceaselessly fiercely<br />
That piece of pasture had turned entire into a sugarcane field<br />
That pasture of my childhood had not been childhood still<br />
Many things are submerged, pushed over and rewrote by the flood of history<br />
How long can it stands straighten up?</p>
<p>Feel free to lifts up the head, stubbornness!<br />
It can’t flee<br />
I see indistinctly it bear the senile, dot and dot of old age speckle, deep wrinkle and white hair on the back<br />
Even hear indistinctly its coughs, melancholy and sigh<br />
It must be hang the dusk and chill winter on its body slowly!<br />
Only can shines not to surrender to take the place of lift up the head, stubbornness@</p>
