<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>看新闻学英语：美国冒险家抵达雪梨</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p><B>American Adventurer Arrives in Sydney</B><br />美国冒险家抵达雪梨<br /><embed src="http://www.youmaker.com/v.swf" width="450" b="358" bgcolor="#FFFFFF" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer" allowFullScreen="true"flashvars="file=http://www.youmaker.com/video/v%3Fid%3D65cdd01dfade486a83411e9024dc275a001%26nu%3Dnu&#038;showdigits=true&#038;overstretch=fit&#038;autostart=false&#038;rotatetime=12&#038;linkfromdisplay=false&#038;repeat=list&#038;shuffle=false&#038;&#038;showfsbutton=false&#038;fsreturnpage=&#038;fullscreenpage=" /> </p> <p>本新闻影音出处：新唐人电视台英语新闻<br />http://english.ntdtv.com/ntdtv_en/ns_life/2010-04-02/567390408817.md#1</p> <p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1. <B>cycle</B> ：v. 骑自行车<br />2.<B> rowing </B> ：n. 划船<br />3. <B>kayak </B> ：v. 划独木舟<br />4. <B>summit</B> ：n. 山顶、顶点 </p> <p>Nearly three years into Erden Eruc&#8217;s self-propelled journey around the globe, he <B>cycled</B>to the Sydney Opera House on Friday, greeted by friends and well-wishers.<br />埃尔登?伊鲁克展开“自力”环游世界的旅程已经将近3年，周五他骑着自行车抵达雪梨歌剧院，他的朋友以及许多为他加油打气的人们，都在此欢迎他的到来。 </p> <p>Erden has spent 312 days <B>rowing </B> across the Pacific from California to Papua New Guinea, walking the Kokoda Track, then cycling down the east coast of Australia.<br />埃尔登花了312天，从加州划船穿越太平洋到巴布新几内亚，步行越过科科达小径，然后沿着澳大利亚东海岸骑车南下。 </p> <p>48-year-old Eruc was greeted by applause at the Opera House, where he showed a map to an awed crowd and explained how he got to Sydney.<br />48岁的伊鲁克在歌剧院外受到热烈的掌声和欢迎，然后他在这里向佩服他的人群展示一张地图，并解释他是如何来到雪梨的。 </p> <p>[Erden Eruc, Adventurer]:<br />&#8220;And then I<B> kayaked</B>to Oro Bay, walked south using the Kokoda Track to Port Moresby, coast to coast, from Solomon Sea to the Coral Sea and then I was on my row boat again for 33 days to get to Cape York. I reached Escape River and after that I kayaked south for about three and a half weeks to Cooktown and I have been on the bicycle since.&#8221; <br />探险家伊鲁克说：“然后我划着小船来到欧罗湾，接着向南穿越科科达小径来到了摩尔斯比港，从所罗门海到珊瑚海，然后再划着我的小船航行33天来到了约克角。我抵达Escape河之后，我又向南划了约三个半星期来到库克镇，然后一直到现在都是骑着自行车。“ </p> <p>He says he plans to spend a few days relaxing with friends. Sydney is only one stop in his long journey, which he said he is making up as he goes along.<br />他说，他计划花几天时间和朋友放松一下。雪梨只是他计划中漫长旅程的一站。 </p> <p>[Erden Eruc, Adventurer]:<br />&#8220;I am very happy. I have covered an enormous distance. It has taken me a while to get here, but finally I made it. And I&#8217;m here.&#8221; <br />伊鲁克说：“我非常高兴，我的旅程已涵盖非常长的距离。我花了好一段时间才来到这里，但我最后终于做到了。而且我现在就在这里。” </p> <p>He will also climb the highest<B> summits</B>in six different continents including the 7,310 feet Mt. Kosciusko in Australia. <br />他还登上六个不同大陆的最高峰，包括在澳洲7,310英尺的科修斯山。 </p> <p>5.<B> expedition</B> : n. 远征<br />6.<B> struggle</B> ：v. 奋斗、争取<br />7. <B>self-sufficiency</B> ：adj. 自给自足的<br />8. <B>tribute</B> : n. 致敬<br />9. <B>daunting</B> ：adj. 严峻的、令人却步的<br />10.<B> kayaking</B> : n. 皮艇运动<br />11.<B> scale</B> ：v. 攀登 <br />12. <B>steep</B> ：adj. 陡峭的</p> <p>Eruc’s self-powered <B>expedition </B> is supported by his non-profit organization called &#8216;Around-n-Over&#8217;. Its mission is to assist poor communities in their <B>struggle</B>towards <B>self-sufficiency</B>by providing basic educational aid and resources.<br />伊鲁克这趟自力远征，都是由他的非营利组织Around-n-Over所赞助的。基金会的使命是藉由提供基本的教育援助和资源，协助贫困的社区达到自给自足。 </p> <p>Eruc&#8217;s organization &#8220;Around-n-Over&#8221; is a<B> tribute </B> to his friend Goran Kropp who encouraged him to take the journey but died while rock climbing with Eruc eight years ago.<br />伊鲁克的组织“Around-n-Over”是为了纪念伊鲁克的朋友Goran Kropp而成立的，他鼓励伊鲁克展开这项旅程，但他在8年前和伊鲁克攀岩的时候不幸死亡。 </p> <p>The adventurer says his biggest concerns are not the <B>daunting</B>challenges such as <B>kayaking</B>across rough seas and <B>scaling steep </B> mountains, but insects. <br />这位冒险家说，他最大的担忧不是严峻的挑战，如在巨大的风浪中划皮艇或是攀登陡峭的高山，而是昆虫。 </p> <p>13. <B>sandfly</B> ：n. 蚋<br />14. <B>mosquito</B> ：n. 蚊子<br />15.<B> encounter </B> : v. 遭遇<br />16. <B>crocodile </B> : n. 鳄鱼<br />17.<B> shark</B>: n. 鲨鱼<br />18. <B>afloat</B> ：adj. 漂浮的</p> <p>[Erden Eruc, Adventurer]:<br />&#8220;The most difficult part of this journey I think were the <B>sandflies </B> and the <B>mosquitoes </B> that I <B>encountered</B> . Also the <B>crocodile</B>danger that I had while sea kayaking the far north-east Cape York.&#8221;<br />“在旅程中最困难的部分，我觉得是那些我遇到的蚋和蚊子。鳄鱼也是相当危险，我曾在Cape York东北部的海上划皮艇时遇到过。”</p> <p>He&#8217;s also encountered <B>sharks</B> , and had to cook at sea while spending 312 days <B>afloat</B> . He has made a record as the man who has spent the most time at sea in a rowboat. <br />他也曾遇到鲨鱼，而且在312天海上漂流的时候，他也必须在海上做饭吃。他还创下以划桨船待在海上最长时间的纪录。 </p> <p>Eruc says he is unsure when he will make it back to his home in Seattle.<br />伊鲁克说，他不确定他什么时候会回他在西雅图的家。<br />@* </p> <p> <p><p> <font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
