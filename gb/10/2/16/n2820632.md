<tr><td VALIGN=TOP width="626"><h2 align=center>看新闻学英语(236)：熔岩雕塑品</h2>

<h6></h6>
<hr>
<p><B>Filipino Artist Turns Lava into Sculptures</B><br />菲律宾艺术家将熔岩变成雕塑品</p>
<p><embed src="http://www.youmaker.com/v.swf" width="450" b="358" bgcolor="#FFFFFF" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" pluginspage="http://www.macromedia.com/go/getflashplayer" allowFullScreen="true"flashvars="file=http://www.youmaker.com/video/v%3Fid%3Dfbdab5938adc41ef939ee938b3b9385e001%26nu%3Dnu&#038;showdigits=true&#038;overstretch=fit&#038;autostart=false&#038;rotatetime=12&#038;linkfromdisplay=false&#038;repeat=list&#038;shuffle=false&#038;&#038;showfsbutton=false&#038;fsreturnpage=&#038;fullscreenpage=" /> <br />本新闻影音出处：新唐人电视台英语</p>
<p><B>【新闻关键字】</B><br />1.<B> lava</B>：n.熔岩<br />2. <B>sculptures </B>：n.雕刻品<br />3. <B>depict</B>：v. 描绘、雕出<br />4. <B>erupt</B>：v.爆发<br />5. <B>renowned</B>：adj.有名的<br />6.<B> exhibit</B>：v.展示<br />7.<B> cater</B>：v.迎合<br />8.<B> establishment</B>：n.机关<br />9. <B>flock</B>：v.聚集<br />10.<B> hazardous</B>：adj.有危险的</p>
<p>Rock<B> sculptor</B> Vicente Ajero is turning volcanic materials into sculptures<B> depicting </B>popular images. <br />岩石雕刻家Vicente Ajero将火山所形成的石材，雕刻成受欢迎的雕塑作品。</p>
<p>Ajero uses lava from the Mayon Volcano washed away by the rains down the slopes and to nearby towns.<br />Ajero所利用的熔岩是来自经由雨水沿着马永火山斜坡冲刷下来而得，然后搬运到附近城镇的。</p>
<p>[Vicente Ajero, Rock Sculptor]:<br />“I can&#8217;t say that I&#8217;m happy about the volcano <B>erupting</B>. The lava is dangerous. But these are the materials that I need to create my art works.”<br />Vicente Ajero，岩石雕刻家说: <br />“我并不是说我很高兴火山爆发，熔岩是有危险性的。但是这些喷发物却是创作我的艺术作品所需要的材料。”</p>
<p>It’s been nine years since Ajero began his volcano-inspired craft.<br />Ajero从事这项由火山所启发的技艺已有9年。</p>
<p> [Vicente Ajero, Rock Sculptor]: <br />“I dream of becoming famous. I didn&#8217;t reach fame by being a singer. Maybe by sculpting these lava rocks, I will be<B> renowned</B>.”<br />Vicente Ajero，岩石雕刻家说: <br />“我梦想能够扬名立万，虽然我当歌手并没有成名，但是我或许可以藉由雕刻这些熔岩而出名。”</p>
<p>Ajero<B> exhibits</B> his art works in a small shop near the Mayon Volcano.<br />Ajero在马永火山附近的一间小店里展示他的艺术品。</p>
<p>His souvenir items are worth $2 to article content begin -->1 U.S. dollars and his large sculptures sell for up to $320 U.S. dollars. He<B> caters</B> to foreign and local tourists as well as business<B> establishments</B>.<br />他的纪念品每项价值2~11美元不等，同时其大型雕刻品可卖到高达320美元。他迎合了外国和本地的观光客以及一些企业团体。</p>
<p>[Honesto Deoda, Tourist]:<br />“His art works are impressive. He&#8217;s found a way to use waste materials and bring them back to life.” <br /> Honesto Deoda，观光客说: <br />“他的作品令人印象深刻，因为他发现了使用废料的方法，并且重新赋予新的生命在其中。”</p>
<p>The near perfect-cone-shaped Mayon Volcano draws thousands of tourists to the Bicol region. Many locals make a living selling souvenirs depicting the volcano. <br />马永火山以其近乎完美的圆锥形，吸引了数千名观光客到Bicol地区来。许多当地居民就以贩卖刻画火山的纪念品为生。</p>
<p>Despite the<B> hazardous</B> eruption warning, locals and foreigners continue<B> flocking</B> to Albany province to watch the volcano.<br />尽管有危险爆发的警告，许多当地居民和外国人仍继续聚集到Albany省去观赏火山。</p>
<p>Mayon is the most active of 22 volcanoes in the country, having erupted more than 50 times in the past four centuries. <br />马永火山是菲律宾境内22座火山当中最为活跃的，在过去的四个世纪中已经爆发超过50次。<br />@*<br /> <font color=#ffffff>(http://www.dajiyuan.com)</font></p>
